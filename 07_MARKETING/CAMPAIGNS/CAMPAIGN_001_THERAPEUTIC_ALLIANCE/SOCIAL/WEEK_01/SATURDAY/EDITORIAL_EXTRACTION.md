## Structural Elements

### Strongest Opening Hook
"Most clinicians work the opposite way. They come in with a plan. They apply technique. They impose change. The most skilled clinicians work differently. They spend time observing first. They match the patient's current state before redirecting. The paradox: working less hard creates better outcomes."

**Why:** Reframes clinical excellence from effort/technique to observation/responsiveness. Immediately differentiates the article's perspective.

---

### Key Insight
**Primary:** Wu Wei is not passivity. It's intelligent non-imposition: observe, match, respond. Don't force.

**Supporting insights:**
- Force creates nervous system resistance
- Observation generates reliable information
- Matching precedes leading
- Waiting is data-gathering
- Effortless is more efficient

---

### Best Quote
**Primary quote:** "Working less hard creates better outcomes."

**Secondary quotes:**
- "Don't impose. Observe. Listen. Match. Respond."
- "Technique applied without observation creates friction. Technique applied in response to what you're observing creates flow."
- "You're no longer fighting. You're cooperating."

---

### Practical Takeaway
**The Wu Wei Sequence:**
1. First 2 minutes: Observe (don't immediately treat; watch breathing, notice posture, feel tissue state)
2. Next 2 minutes: Match (gently contact their pattern; let them feel understood)
3. Then: Respond (only intervene if tissue is ready; only when permission exists)
