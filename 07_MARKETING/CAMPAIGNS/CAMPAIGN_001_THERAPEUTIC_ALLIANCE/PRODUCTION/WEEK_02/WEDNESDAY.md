## Carousel Narrative

This is one story across five slides, not five isolated quotes.

**Story Arc:**
1. Hook: Introduce the concept of "beard" (something you broadcast unconsciously)
2. Definition: What a beard actually is (nervous system signal)
3. Specificity: Examples of different beards
4. Reframe: What matters is the gap between intention and reception
5. Reflection: Invitation to self-audit + CTA to article

**Tone:** Self-aware, humble, clinically grounded. Not shame-based. Not performative.

**Design Standard:** Premium editorial healthcare photography style
- Close, observational framing (not wide clinic scenes)
- Warm natural window light
- Authentic professional boundaries
- Linen, warm white, oak, muted navy, sage, natural skin tones
- No forced smiles or mystical effects
- No Chinese characters

## Editorial Notes

**Purpose:** Introduce the "beard" concept as a memorable, personal framework for self-audit.

**Key Principles:**
- Humility: "You may not realize"
- No shame: "Not about performing calmness"
- Clinical grounding: Tie to nervous system reading
- Accessible: Use clear examples
- Reflective: End with self-audit question

**Voice Safeguards:**
- Do NOT shame clinicians for being busy or naturally direct
- Do NOT suggest appearance-based judgment matters
- Do NOT use clichés like "unlock" or "transform"
- Do NOT create false binaries (calm vs. rushed)
- Do use observable signals
- Do use "your patient's nervous system reads"
- Do invite self-reflection without prescription
