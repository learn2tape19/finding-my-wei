## FRIDAY CLINICAL FOUNDATION

The Two-Breath Pause is performed after initial contact meets tissue resistance.

**The approved clinical sequence:**

1. Match the tissue's resistance.
2. Maintain contact. Do not deepen pressure or push through the resistance.
3. Wait through two natural, unforced breaths.
4. Notice what changes—and what does not.

**Clinical principle:** A change contributes information. It does not, by itself, determine the cause.

**Friday completes the Week 3 arc:**
- Tuesday: Assessment begins before the formal test
- Wednesday: Clinician presence shapes observable findings
- Thursday: Assessment findings arise within specific conditions
- Friday: One practical technique—the pause itself—demonstrates the principle

## CLINICAL LANGUAGE GOVERNANCE

**Preserved throughout Friday package:**

- The pause is a **reflection and observation practice**, not a diagnostic test
- "Match the resistance" — clinician adapts to patient tissue, not vice versa
- "Do not deepen pressure" — explicit boundary against forcing the tissue
- "Wait through two natural breaths" — unforced, patient-centered timing
- "Notice what changes—and what does not" — clinical observation without premature interpretation
- **A change contributes information. It does not determine the cause.** — The core interpretive qualification

**Not included in Friday (per clinical boundaries):**

- Contact-free observation framing
- Claim that the pause itself is therapeutic or curative
- Claim that a change after the pause identifies its cause
- Characterization of the pause as a diagnostic test or tool
- Assumption that deepening pressure after two breaths is appropriate
