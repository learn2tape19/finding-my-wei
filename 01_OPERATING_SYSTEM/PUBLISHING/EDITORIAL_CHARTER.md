# Editorial Charter

## Authority

This charter is issued by the Founder of Finding My Wei and represents permanent institutional policy for all publishing operations.

Effective: July 9, 2026

The Editorial Board operates under Founder authority and reports to the Founder.

Final publication authority rests with the Founder.

## Mission

The Editorial Board exists to ensure every publication produced by Finding My Wei is:

- Scientifically responsible
- Intellectually honest
- Clinically grounded
- Beautifully written
- Consistent with the voice of Finding My Wei
- Worthy of becoming part of a lasting intellectual estate

## Permanent Principles

### 1. Editorial Standards Are Cumulative

Every lesson learned through publication, peer review, Founder feedback, or real-world reader response becomes part of this institution's standards.

The same editorial mistake should never be repeated twice.

Future publications inherit all previous improvements automatically.

### 2. Quality Over Volume

The institution prioritizes lasting intellectual contribution over publication frequency.

A smaller body of exceptional work is preferable to a large body of mediocre work.

Every publication must justify its existence by advancing thinking or deepening understanding.

### 3. Voice as Institutional Signature

All Finding My Wei publications should sound like they come from the same institution, guided by the same principles.

The voice is consistent across Project Atlas, campaigns, newsletters, books, and research.

Variations may occur (different author perspectives, specific campaign needs) but should be intentional and documented.

### 4. Scientific Integrity as Non-Negotiable

The institution's credibility depends on careful, honest handling of evidence.

Claims must be proportional to supporting evidence.

Speculation must be labeled as such.

Neuroscience must never be overstated.

When doubt exists, it should be stated plainly.

### 5. Reader as Trusted Colleague

Writing should speak to intelligent, experienced clinicians as respected colleagues.

Not as people who need to be convinced.

Not as people who need to be taught basic concepts.

The writing should invite reflection, not prescribe behavior.

## Scope

The Editorial Board governs:

- Project Atlas publications (papers, research, investigations)
- Campaign articles (email, social, web)
- The Tao of Clinical Touch (books, essays, doctrines)
- Learn2Tape educational content published under Finding My Wei standards
- Sidekick Air publications
- StitchCore documentation
- White papers and research reports
- Long-form essays and investigations
- Website copy representing institutional voice
- Newsletter content
- LinkedIn articles
- Professional presentations
- Educational handouts
- Any publication carrying the Finding My Wei or Tao institutional brand

The Editorial Board does NOT govern:

- Personal social media posts
- Casual team communication
- Internal working documents not intended for publication
- Founder personal writing (though Founder may request editorial review)

## Responsibilities

The Editorial Board is responsible for:

### Standards Creation and Maintenance

- Creating clear, specific editorial standards
- Updating standards based on publication learnings
- Documenting institutional voice and principles
- Maintaining scientific integrity guidelines
- Establishing style and formatting conventions

### Pre-Publication Review

- Reviewing drafts for voice consistency
- Verifying scientific accuracy and appropriate claims
- Checking adherence to editorial standards
- Providing constructive feedback for improvement
- Preparing drafts for Founder review

### Quality Assurance

- Conducting final verification before publication
- Ensuring all checklists are completed
- Confirming repository and git procedures are correct
- Verifying publication is accurately stored and accessible

### Continuous Improvement

- Gathering feedback from readers and stakeholders
- Documenting lessons learned from every publication
- Identifying patterns in what resonates and what doesn't
- Updating standards to incorporate new learning

### Institutional Memory

- Maintaining the archive of editorial decisions and reasoning
- Creating precedent and context for future publications
- Ensuring consistency across time and authors
- Protecting institutional voice from drift

## Relationship to Founder

The Founder is the final authority on all publications.

Editorial Board review prepares publications for Founder review by:

- Ensuring technical compliance with standards
- Catching voice inconsistencies
- Identifying scientific or factual issues
- Providing recommendations and context

Founder review is the gate for:

- Strategic alignment
- Institutional voice and positioning
- Final quality judgment
- Publication decision

The Editorial Board does not make final publication decisions.

The Founder does.

## Relationship to Project Atlas

Project Atlas is the primary intellectual initiative of Finding My Wei.

Atlas publications receive priority editorial attention and extended review cycles.

All Atlas publications must meet:

- General Editorial Standards (see EDITORIAL_STANDARDS.md)
- Scientific Integrity Standards (see SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY_STANDARD.md)
- Atlas-specific scientific rigor requirements
- Founder strategic approval

Atlas publications contribute directly to the institutional intellectual estate and are treated as permanent institutional records.

## Relationship to Campaigns

Campaigns serve both marketing and educational purposes.

Campaign publications must sound like Finding My Wei and represent institutional thinking.

Campaign articles should NOT:

- Use manipulation tactics
- Oversimplify complex ideas
- Exaggerate claims
- Prioritize urgency over accuracy

Campaign articles SHOULD:

- Advance reader thinking
- Model institutional voice
- Respect reader intelligence
- Contribute something worth reading and remembering

When campaign imperatives conflict with editorial standards, the Founder determines priority.

## Relationship to Books

Books are major institutional publications and receive extended editorial treatment.

Books must meet all general editorial standards plus:

- Extended scientific review (multiple passes)
- Structural consistency across chapters
- Coherent voice throughout
- Substantial contribution to institutional knowledge
- Enduring value beyond immediate context

Books are treated as permanent institutional records and are audited regularly to ensure they continue to represent current institutional thinking.

## Relationship to Research

All research-based publications must clearly distinguish:

1. Established scientific evidence
2. Current scientific understanding
3. Clinical observation and experience
4. Professional interpretation
5. Personal reflection

Research publications carry the highest scientific rigor requirements.

See SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY_STANDARD.md for specific guidance.

## Publication Authority

### Who Can Initiate Publications?

- Founder
- Editorial Board members (with Founder approval)
- Project team members (with Editorial Board and Founder approval)
- External contributors (with Editorial Board and Founder approval)

### Who Reviews?

- Editorial Board reviews all drafts
- Founder reviews all publications before final approval

### Who Publishes?

- Founder approves publication
- Designated publication team executes (web, social, email, print, etc.)

### Who Maintains?

- Editorial Board monitors for needed updates
- Founder approves significant revisions
- Team maintains repository records

## Standards Are Living Doctrine

This charter is permanent.

The specific standards it governs are living documents that improve over time.

Changes to Editorial Standards should be:

- Evidence-based (grounded in publication experience)
- Documented (recorded with rationale)
- Cumulative (never reverting to lower standards)
- Transparent (visible in git history)

No lesson learned should be forgotten.

No standard should be weakened.

The institution becomes a better writer with every publication.
