# Editorial Audit Report

**Initial Assessment of Existing Publications**

**Date:** July 9, 2026

**Auditor:** Editorial Board

**Status:** In Progress

---

## Purpose

This audit assesses existing Finding My Wei publications against the new Editorial Standards established July 9, 2026.

The audit is NOT a rewrite request.

It is an assessment of current alignment and a prioritized roadmap for bringing publications into full compliance over time.

---

## Audit Categories

Each publication is categorized by the work needed to fully align with standards:

### Category A: Already Aligned
No significant revisions needed. Publication meets current standards.

### Category B: Light Revision
Minor updates to voice, formatting, or punctuation. Under 5% of content needs change.

### Category C: Moderate Revision
Some sections need rewriting for voice or flow. Approximately 20-30% of content needs attention.

### Category D: Significant Revision
Major restructuring or rewriting needed. More than 30% of content needs work.

### Category E: Scientific Refinement
Scientific accuracy or claim proportionality issues. Scientific review required before republication.

### Category F: Voice Refinement
Significant voice inconsistency with standards. Needs rewriting for tone and consistency.

### Category G: Structural Refinement
Flow, organization, or reader experience needs improvement. Significant restructuring needed.

---

## Process

1. **Assessment** — Audit each publication against standards
2. **Categorization** — Assign category based on work needed
3. **Prioritization** — Rank by impact and effort
4. **Roadmap** — Create timeline for updates
5. **Execution** — Complete updates in priority order

## Decision: Retroactive Compliance

**Policy Decision:** 

All publications created BEFORE July 9, 2026 are not required to comply with all new standards.

However:

- **Major publications** (books, flagship papers, core research) should be updated
- **Active publications** (currently promoted or referenced) should be aligned
- **Archived publications** can remain as historical record

**Rationale:**

- Standards were created AFTER many publications were written
- Retroactively rewriting everything would pause new work
- Strategic prioritization of effort is more effective
- New publications set the standard going forward

---

## Compliance Going Forward

**Permanent Policy:**

All publications created AFTER July 9, 2026 must meet full Editorial Standards before publication.

No publication proceeds without:
- ✓ Scientific Review
- ✓ Editorial Board Review
- ✓ Founder Approval
- ✓ Checklist completion
- ✓ Post-Flight Verification
