## The Complete Journey

Every publication follows this 15-stage lifecycle from conception to archived completion.

```
1. IDEA
   └─ Topic identified, question formulated

2. RESEARCH
   └─ Evidence gathered, sources documented

3. ATLAS INVESTIGATION (Optional)
   └─ For Project Atlas publications only

4. DRAFT
   └─ First complete version written

5. EDITORIAL REVIEW
   └─ Voice, structure, flow verified

6. SCIENTIFIC REVIEW
   └─ Claims, evidence, interpretation verified

7. FOUNDER APPROVAL
   └─ Final authority on voice and strategy

8. CANONICAL ARTICLE UPDATED
   └─ Article written/approved in /BLOG location

9. PRODUCTION ASSETS UPDATED
   └─ Metadata, social copy, specs in /PRODUCTION

10. GIT COMMIT
    └─ Changes staged and committed

11. GIT PUSH
    └─ Pushed to origin/main

12. READY TO PUBLISH
    └─ All pre-flight checks passed

13. PUBLISHED
    └─ Article live on intended platform

14. EDITORIAL REFLECTION
    └─ Lessons captured, feedback reviewed

15. INSTITUTIONAL LEARNING
    └─ Patterns identified, standards updated
```

---

## Stage Definitions

### Stage 1: Idea
**Owner:** Author  
**Duration:** Variable  
**Deliverable:** Topic statement or research question  
**Criteria:** Clear, specific, worth exploring

### Stage 2: Research
**Owner:** Author  
**Duration:** Variable  
**Deliverable:** Research notes, sources, findings  
**Criteria:** Sufficient evidence gathered, gaps identified

### Stage 3: Atlas Investigation (Optional)
**Owner:** Project Atlas  
**Duration:** Variable  
**Deliverable:** Investigation report  
**Criteria:** Relevant to atlas thesis, properly documented

### Stage 4: Draft
**Owner:** Author  
**Duration:** 1-2 weeks typical  
**Deliverable:** Complete first draft  
**Criteria:** All sections present, ready for review

### Stage 5: Editorial Review
**Owner:** Editorial Board  
**Duration:** 2-3 days typical  
**Deliverable:** Editorial feedback, revision suggestions  
**Criteria:** Voice consistency, structure, flow verified

### Stage 6: Scientific Review
**Owner:** Scientific Reviewer  
**Duration:** 3-5 days typical  
**Deliverable:** Scientific verification, claim audit  
**Criteria:** Evidence proportional, interpretation clear, no unsupported claims

### Stage 7: Founder Approval
**Owner:** Founder  
**Duration:** Variable  
**Deliverable:** Final approval or requested revisions  
**Criteria:** Aligns with institutional voice and strategy

### Stage 8: Canonical Article Updated
**Owner:** Author  
**Duration:** 1-2 days  
**Deliverable:** Final article in /BLOG location  
**Criteria:** All feedback incorporated, ready for platform

### Stage 9: Production Assets Updated
**Owner:** Production Team  
**Duration:** 1-2 days  
**Deliverable:** Metadata, social copy, specs in /PRODUCTION  
**Criteria:** All specs complete, ready for publishing

### Stage 10: Git Commit
**Owner:** Publishing Team  
**Duration:** < 1 hour  
**Deliverable:** Committed changes with clear message  
**Criteria:** Pre-flight check passed, commit message clear

### Stage 11: Git Push
**Owner:** Publishing Team  
**Duration:** < 1 hour  
**Deliverable:** Changes pushed to origin/main  
**Criteria:** Push successful, origin updated

### Stage 12: Ready to Publish
**Owner:** Editorial Board  
**Duration:** < 1 hour  
**Deliverable:** Post-flight verification passed  
**Criteria:** All checks completed, publication approved

### Stage 13: Published
**Owner:** Publishing Team  
**Duration:** < 1 hour  
**Deliverable:** Article live on intended platform  
**Criteria:** Content accessible, metadata correct, links working

### Stage 14: Editorial Reflection
**Owner:** Editorial Board + Author  
**Duration:** 1 week  
**Deliverable:** Reflection document, feedback summary  
**Criteria:** Lessons captured, patterns identified

### Stage 15: Institutional Learning
**Owner:** Editorial Board  
**Duration:** 1-2 weeks  
**Deliverable:** Standards updates, documentation improvements  
**Criteria:** Learning committed to repository, incorporated into standards

---

## Expected Timelines

### Fast Track
Research → Draft → Reviews → Founder → Publication
**Duration:** 2-3 weeks (for urgent content)

### Standard Track
Research → Draft → Reviews → Revision → Founder → Publication
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks (typical)

### Major Publication (Paper, Book)
Research → Investigation → Draft → Multiple Reviews → Revision → Founder → Publication
**Duration:** 2-3 months (complex, major work)

---

## Governance

Every stage has clear ownership and defined criteria for advancement.

No publication advances without completing all prior stages.

Stages cannot be skipped; they must occur in order.
