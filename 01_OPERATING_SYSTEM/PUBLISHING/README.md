# Editorial Board

## Purpose

The Editorial Board exists to protect the quality, integrity, voice, scientific accuracy, and long-term value of every publication produced by Finding My Wei.

This is not campaign-specific work. This is a permanent operational department within the institution.

The responsibility of the Editorial Board is to ensure that every future publication contributes to the intellectual estate rather than merely adding more content.

## Why It Exists

Finding My Wei publishes to advance clinical thinking and to create a lasting intellectual estate. Quality matters more than volume.

The Editorial Board exists because:

- **Voice consistency matters.** Readers should feel like they're hearing from the same institutional perspective across all publications.
- **Scientific integrity is non-negotiable.** The institution's credibility depends on careful, honest handling of evidence and claims.
- **Excellence compounds.** When standards are explicitly captured, every future publication inherits those improvements automatically.
- **Editorial decisions should not be made in isolation.** Shared standards create accountability and prevent drift.

## Publication Workflow

Every publication follows this sequence:

1. **Idea** — A topic or question worth exploring
2. **Research** — Gathering evidence, reading, clinical reflection
3. **Draft** — Initial writing, following the editorial principles
4. **Scientific Review** — Verify evidence, separate claims from interpretation
5. **Editorial Board Review** — Check voice, flow, consistency, adherence to standards
6. **Founder Review** — Final authority on strategy and institutional voice
7. **Revision** — Apply feedback, strengthen weak sections
8. **Repository Commit** — Stage and commit to canonical repo
9. **GitHub Push** — Push to origin/main
10. **Publication** — Publish to designated platform
11. **Editorial Reflection** — Document what was learned
12. **Standards Update** — Update Editorial Standards accordingly

See **EDITORIAL_WORKFLOW.md** for detailed guidance.

## Relationship to Founder Review

The Founder has final authority on all publications.

The Editorial Board prepares publications and surfaces recommendations before Founder review.

The Founder review is the gate for institutional voice and strategic alignment.

Editorial Board review is the gate for quality, consistency, and adherence to standards.

Both reviews are required.

## Relationship to Project Atlas

Project Atlas publications must meet all Editorial Board standards.

Atlas publications must also meet the specific scientific rigor standards defined in **SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY_STANDARD.md**.

The Editorial Audit Report includes a section specific to Atlas publication readiness.

## Relationship to Campaigns

Campaign articles must sound like Finding My Wei.

Campaign articles should follow the same editorial workflow as other publications.

Campaign-specific variations in voice or style must be documented and approved by Editorial Board and Founder.

## Relationship to Books

Books follow the same Editorial Board standards but with extended review cycles and greater depth.

Major publications (books, white papers) require additional scientific review and editorial passes.

## Relationship to Research

All research-based writing must clearly distinguish:

- Established scientific evidence
- Current understanding
- Clinical observation
- Interpretation
- Personal reflection

See **SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY_STANDARD.md** for detailed requirements.

## Core Principle

The institution publishes fewer things.

But publishes better things.

Every sentence should leave the reader feeling more curious than certain.

That is where learning begins.
