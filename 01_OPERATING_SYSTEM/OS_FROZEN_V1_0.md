## Governance Principle

The Operating System provides complete governance infrastructure for:

- Editorial standards and voice consistency
- Scientific integrity and evidence handling
- Publishing architecture and source-of-truth clarity
- Publication lifecycle and status tracking
- Institutional memory and decision logging
- Continuous improvement through real-world learning

No further systemic improvements shall be designed speculatively.

All future improvements must be driven by evidence from actual publication work.

---

## Why v1.0 is Complete

1. **Editorial Standards:** Comprehensive voice, craft, and scientific integrity standards defined.

2. **Publishing Architecture:** Single-source principle eliminates ambiguity about article sources.

3. **Workflow Definition:** Complete lifecycle from idea through archive is documented.

4. **Institutional Memory:** Decisions logged with reasoning; institutional knowledge captures learning.

5. **Quality Assurance:** Pre-flight and post-flight checks ensure consistency.

6. **Continuous Improvement:** Mechanism established for standards to evolve based on real-world publishing.

7. **Governance:** Clear authority structure, responsibilities defined, enforcement mechanisms established.

Everything needed to publish with discipline and consistency is now in place.

## Unfrozen Only By

Any future OS changes require:

1. **Evidence:** Demonstration that OS v1.0 creates material limitation in real-world publishing
2. **Documentation:** Clear articulation of the limitation and required change
3. **Founder Authority:** Explicit Founder approval for any unfreezing
4. **Institutional Memory:** Amendment recorded in EDITORIAL_DECISION_LOG.md

The standard is: demonstrate material limitation, then evolve.

Not: speculate about improvements, then implement.
