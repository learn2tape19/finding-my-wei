# Core Principles

Living standards and frameworks that govern decision-making, evaluation, and work across all projects.

---

## On Knowledge & Learning

**All knowledge is contextual.** A framework true in one context may be false in another. We document context alongside conclusions.

**Reflection compounds.** Regular review—weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually—produces exponential improvement in judgment.

**Teaching deepens understanding.** If you cannot teach it clearly, you do not understand it well enough.

**Patterns emerge from repetition.** The system captures recurring patterns so future work can learn from past work.

---

## On Writing & Communication

**Clear writing is clear thinking.** Confusion in writing signals confusion in thought.

**One idea per document.** Modular thinking leads to modular writing. Long documents confuse navigation.

**Link generously.** The value of a knowledge system is in connections, not storage.

**Name things well.** Good names are permanent. Renaming breaks history.

---

## On Decisions

**Document the reasoning, not just the decision.** Why you chose A over B matters more than that you chose A.

**Include dissenting views.** Record what you thought might be wrong with your choice.

**State assumptions explicitly.** Every decision rests on assumptions. Make them visible.

**Revisit quarterly.** The best decisions become wrong in different contexts. Regular review catches drift.

---

## On Publishing & Visibility

**Work in public.** Ideas refined in conversation with others are stronger than private thinking.

**Publish before you're ready.** Perfect is the enemy of good. Publish at 80% and improve from feedback.

**Attribution matters.** Credit collaborators, acknowledge sources, honor the thinking that came before.

**Evergreen first.** Write for people reading this in five years, not people reading it today.

---

## On Collaboration

**Complementary skills > individual excellence.** Hire/partner with people who fill gaps, not people who replicate you.

**Clarity over harmony.** Disagreement resolved is stronger than artificial agreement.

**Roles matter.** Claude does architecture. ChatGPT does strategy. Drew makes final calls. Clear roles prevent chaos.

**Process scales.** What works with one person should work with five, if the process is right.

---

## On Creativity

**Constraints enable creation.** Unlimited options paralyze. Good constraints focus energy.

**Steal like an artist.** Learn from what works, improve it, make it your own. Credit the source.

**Iteration beats inspiration.** 50 iterations of a good idea beats one stroke of genius.

**Rest is part of the work.** Thinking requires downtime. Protect space for reflection.

---

## On Business & Impact

**Sustainable > optimized.** We build to last decades, not quarters.

**Owner mindset.** Act like you own it, because in some sense, you do.

**Compound margins.** Small improvements in quality, efficiency, and clarity compound over years.

**Meaning over money.** Do work that matters. Money follows meaning.

---

## On Technology

**Tools serve purpose.** A tool is good if it serves the purpose. GitHub serves permanence. Buffer serves publishing.

**Platform risk is real.** Keep our assets in formats and locations we control. GitHub is permanent. Twitter is temporary.

**Automation over repetition.** If you do it three times, automate it.

**Beautiful systems are sustainable.** Ugly code, ugly processes break down. Make systems worth maintaining.

---

## On Growth

**Deep mastery > breadth.** Become world-class in core skills. Delegate everything else.

**Annual review, not quarterly panic.** Judge yourself over years, not quarters.

**Setbacks are data.** Failures teach more than successes.

**Network compounds.** Each good relationship and collaboration makes the next easier.

---

These principles evolve. New principles emerge from experience. But changes are additions, not overrides. The Constitution is permanent. These principles guide within it.
