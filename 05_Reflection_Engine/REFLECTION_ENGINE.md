# Reflection Engine

The system that asks: **What changed? Why? What principle emerged? Where else does this apply?**

The Reflection Engine is how you extract learning from every experience and feed it back into the operating system.

---

## The Reflection Questions

Every reflection asks the same sequence of questions. These questions transform experience into wisdom.

### Core Reflection Sequence

#### Question 1: What Changed?
- What was true before?
- What is true now?
- What specifically changed?
- Who changed? (You? Others? Both?)
- When did the change become visible?

**Purpose**: Establish the facts. Get specific. Avoid vagueness.

**Output**: Clear statement of what changed, dated

---

#### Question 2: Why Did It Matter?
- What would have happened if nothing had changed?
- What becomes possible now that wasn't before?
- Who benefited most from this change?
- What cost came with this change?
- Why am I taking time to reflect on this?

**Purpose**: Establish significance. Not everything matters equally.

**Output**: Articulation of stakes and impact

---

#### Question 3: What Principle Emerged?
- What timeless truth did I learn?
- How would I describe this to someone with no context?
- What would this be false about? (Find the boundaries)
- Does this contradict any principle I already hold?
- How do I know if this principle is actually true?

**Purpose**: Extract the durable knowledge. Move from "what happened" to "what's always true."

**Output**: 1-3 principles stated as timeless, not situational

---

#### Question 4: Where Else Does This Apply?
- What other projects does this principle affect?
- What other people should know this?
- Where in my work do I see this pattern repeated?
- What research does this connect to?
- What would change if I applied this principle everywhere relevant?

**Purpose**: Multiply the value. One insight that applies in 10 contexts is 10x more valuable.

**Output**: Explicit connections to other work, projects, research, people

---

#### Question 5: Should This Become...?
Ask this for each possible form:

**Research?**
- Should I investigate this more deeply?
- What questions would research answer?
- Is there evidence to find or generate?

**Social Content?**
- Is this useful enough to share with my audience?
- What's the one-tweet version?
- What's the longer thread?
- Where does this belong in my thinking architecture?

**Paper?**
- Is this significant enough for formal writing?
- What journal might publish this?
- What peer review would this survive?
- How does this advance the field?

**Lecture?**
- Is this teachable?
- Who should know this?
- What's the teaching arc?
- How would I structure a talk around this?

**Framework?**
- Is this a reusable framework for decision-making?
- What are the components?
- When would someone use this?
- How do you know if it's working?

**Product?**
- Is there a product hiding inside this insight?
- Who would pay for this?
- What problem does it solve?
- How is it different from existing alternatives?

**Operating Principle?**
- Should this change how we operate?
- Who needs to know this?
- How do we codify it?
- How do we measure if we're living by it?

**Archive?**
- Is this valuable for future reference but not immediately actionable?
- Where should this live for future-you to find?

**Purpose**: Determine emergence. One principle becomes multiple creations.

**Output**: Decision on what this becomes (can be multiple)

---

#### Question 6: What Would I Do Differently?
- Given what I now know, what would I change?
- What do I wish I'd known earlier?
- What was the costliest mistake in this experience?
- What worked that surprised me?
- What do I want to do the same way next time?

**Purpose**: Close the loop. Learning only matters if it changes future behavior.

**Output**: Explicit changes to how you'll operate going forward

## Reflection by Time Horizon

Reflection happens at multiple scales. Different timeframes ask different questions.

### Daily Reflection (5-10 minutes)
**When**: End of day, before sleep  
**Focus**: What surprised me? What did I learn? What will I do differently tomorrow?  
**Output**: Quick journal entry, tomorrow's adjusted priorities  
**Storage**: Personal journal (feeds weekly reflection)

---

### Weekly Reflection (30-45 minutes)
**When**: Friday afternoon or Sunday evening  
**Focus**: What patterns showed up this week? What projects progressed? What principles clarified?  
**Questions**:
- What were the 3 most important things that happened?
- What principle showed up more than once?
- What didn't go as planned? Why?
- What surprised me?
- What's my priority for next week?

**Output**: Weekly reflection document (feeds monthly reflection)  
**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/weekly/`

---

### Monthly Reflection (1-2 hours)
**When**: Last day of month  
**Focus**: What themes emerged? What shifted? What's the meta-pattern?  
**Questions**:
- What were the 5 biggest events this month?
- What principle emerged that's new?
- What principle was reinforced?
- What project moved forward?
- What surprised me most?
- How is this month's work connected?
- What do the daily and weekly reflections add up to?

**Output**: Monthly reflection document (feeds quarterly review)  
**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/monthly/2026-[month].md`

---

### Quarterly Reflection (3-4 hours)
**When**: Last week of quarter  
**Focus**: Big picture progress, principle evolution, operating system refinement  
**Questions**:
- What were the 3 biggest projects/outcomes this quarter?
- What 3 principles became clearer?
- What principle was proven wrong or incomplete?
- How has my way of working evolved?
- What's the meta-learning about my own thinking?
- What should next quarter prioritize based on what I learned?
- How is the operating system itself improving?

**Output**: Quarterly reflection document + operating system updates  
**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/quarterly/Q[N]-2026.md`  
**Action**: Update core principles, [[DECISION_INDEX]], workflows

---

### Annual Reflection (Full day)
**When**: December 28-31 or January 1-2  
**Focus**: Complete year review, major decision points, long-term patterns  
**Questions**:
- What were the 5 most important outcomes?
- What 5 principles did I learn?
- What person/collaboration changed my thinking?
- What failed that taught me the most?
- What succeeded that surprised me?
- How much closer am I to the vision I hold?
- What should the next year prioritize?
- What has the entire year taught me about how I think?

**Output**: Annual reflection document + year-end archive  
**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/annual/2026-year-review.md`  
**Action**: Major operating system updates, principle integration, next-year planning

## How Reflection Feeds the System

Reflections don't end with insights. They feed back into:

**Core Principles** (`02_Core_Principles/`)  
If a reflection generates a new principle, it gets added here.

**Concept Index** (`CONCEPT_INDEX.md`)  
New frameworks and ideas go here for discovery.

**Decision Index** (`DECISION_INDEX.md`)  
Major decisions that emerged from reflection get documented.

**Operating System** (`00_Constitution/`)  
If a reflection suggests the operating system itself should change, it gets integrated.

**Knowledge Graph** (`KNOWLEDGE_GRAPH_ARCHITECTURE.md`)  
New connections discovered during reflection expand the graph.

**Daily Workflow** (`DAILY_WORKFLOW.md`)  
If reflection suggests process changes, they get codified here.

## Reflection Discipline

### Rules

1. **Reflection is mandatory**  
   Not optional. Built into the daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual rhythm.

2. **Honesty required**  
   Reflections are most valuable when they're ruthlessly honest about what didn't work.

3. **Specific, not vague**  
   "I learned a lot" is not reflection. "I learned that permission requires nervous system congruence" is.

4. **Connected, not isolated**  
   Every reflection explicitly connects to other work, projects, people, research.

5. **Actionable, not theoretical**  
   Reflection must change something — a decision, a principle, a behavior, a process.

---

## Purpose

Reflection transforms **experience into wisdom**.

Without reflection, you repeat the same year 20 times.  
With reflection, each year builds on the last.

The Reflection Engine makes this compounding automatic and systematic.
