# Daily Workflow

The operating rhythm that powers Finding My Wei. How every day flows through the system.

---

## The Operating Rhythm

Work happens at multiple scales: daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual.

Each scale has specific work. Together they compound.

---

## Daily Rhythm (Monday-Friday)

### Morning (First 60-90 minutes)

**Purpose**: Activate, clarify, set intention

#### 1. Capture (15 minutes)
- Review overnight captures: emails, messages, notes, thoughts
- What emerged while sleeping?
- What's urgent vs. important?

**Using**: UIP Step 1 (Capture)

#### 2. Review (15 minutes)
- Check yesterday's completed items
- Review today's calendar and commitments
- Check decision points that need attention
- Review ongoing projects

**Using**: Decision Engine, Project status

#### 3. Clarify Intention (15 minutes)
- What are the 3 most important things for today?
- What's blocking progress on current projects?
- What decision needs to be made?

**Output**: Daily priority list (3 main items, everything else is secondary)

#### 4. Research/Input (15-30 minutes)
- Deep read on one topic relevant to current work
- Or respond to one key message/conversation
- Or gather data needed for a decision

**Using**: PROMPT_LIBRARY for research questions

---

### Work Hours (Core Creation Time)

**Purpose**: Deep work on highest-leverage activities

#### Priority Work (2-3 hours)
Work on the 3 most important items identified in morning clarification.

This is protected time. Interruptions only for genuine emergencies.

**Possible work types**:
- Writing/creation (book chapter, course material, paper draft)
- Decision-making (analyzing alternatives, choosing path)
- Research (investigating a question systematically)
- Teaching/content (preparing a lecture, recording video)
- Product development (designing, building, testing)
- Strategic thinking (planning, mapping, framework design)

#### Secondary Work (1-2 hours)
Respond to messages, handle operational items, support collaborations.

This can be done in focus blocks rather than continuous interruption.

---

### Late Afternoon (30-60 minutes)

**Purpose**: Transition, capture learning, prepare for tomorrow

#### 1. Decode (15 minutes)
What happened today? What surprised me? What worked?

Quick notes on:
- What I learned
- What changed
- What's unclear
- What to explore tomorrow

**Using**: UIP Step 2 (Decode)

#### 2. Capture Tomorrow (15 minutes)
- What does tomorrow need?
- What's the one most important thing?
- What decision point is coming?
- What research or input would help?

#### 3. Archive (15 minutes)
- File today's work
- Link to relevant projects
- Update decision records if applicable
- Note what changed

**Using**: UIP Step 10 (Archive)

## Weekly Rhythm (Friday or Sunday)

**Time**: 1-2 hours

**Purpose**: Zoom out, see patterns, prepare for next week

### Weekly Reflection
Use the Reflection Engine.

**Questions**:
- What were the 3 biggest things that happened?
- What principle showed up more than once?
- What didn't go as planned? Why?
- What surprised me?
- What's the priority for next week?

**Output**: Weekly reflection document

**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/weekly/[date].md`

### Connect & Organize
- Update project status
- Update decision records with this week's outcomes
- Connect this week's work to existing concepts
- Link to relevant people/research

### Next Week Preparation
- What are the 5 most important things for next week?
- What decisions need to be made?
- What research or input should happen first?
- What could block progress?

---

## Monthly Rhythm (Last day of month)

**Time**: 2-3 hours

**Purpose**: Identify themes and meta-patterns

### Monthly Reflection
Use the Reflection Engine.

**Questions**:
- What were the 5 biggest events this month?
- What principle emerged that's new?
- What principle was reinforced?
- What project moved forward?
- What surprised me most?
- How is this month's work connected?

**Output**: Monthly reflection document

**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/monthly/2026-[month].md`

### Principle Extraction
- Are new principles emerging from this month's work?
- Should any existing principles be refined?
- What's the meta-pattern across all projects?

### Operating System Refinement
- Should any daily/weekly processes change?
- Is the workflow still serving?
- What tools or templates would help?

**Update**: `02_Core_Principles/CORE_PRINCIPLES.md` if needed

---

## Quarterly Rhythm (Last week of quarter)

**Time**: 4-6 hours (can be a full day or split across 2 days)

**Purpose**: Big picture review and major planning

### Quarterly Reflection
Use the Reflection Engine.

**Questions**:
- What were the 3 biggest outcomes?
- What 3 principles became clearer?
- What principle was proven wrong or incomplete?
- How has my way of working evolved?
- What's the meta-learning about how I think?
- What should next quarter prioritize?
- How is the operating system itself improving?

**Output**: Quarterly reflection document + operating system updates

**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/quarterly/Q[N]-2026.md`

### Major Updates
- Update [[DECISION_INDEX.md]] with major decisions
- Update [[CONCEPT_INDEX.md]] with new frameworks
- Update [[CORE_PRINCIPLES.md]] if principles shifted
- Update project status for all active projects

### Strategic Planning
- What are the 3-5 focus areas for next quarter?
- What projects are starting/ending/shifting?
- What research should happen?
- What products should be created?

### Operating System Assessment
- Is the UIP working? What changes?
- Is reflection happening adequately?
- Are decisions being documented?
- Is the knowledge graph growing healthily?

## Annual Rhythm (December 28 - January 2)

**Purpose**: Complete year review and major system refinement

- What 5 principles did I learn?

- What failed that taught me the most?
- What succeeded that surprised me?
- How much closer am I to my vision?
- What should next year prioritize?
- What has the entire year taught me about how I think?

**Output**: Annual reflection document

**Storage**: `05_Reflection_Engine/annual/2026-year-review.md`

### Complete Year Archive
- Archive all completed projects to `22_Archive/2026/`
- Organize all 2026 documents with full metadata
- Write brief summary of what 2026 accomplished
- Extract the meta-narrative (what was the year about?)

- Does the UIP still work? Refine if needed.
- Are all 10 steps working?
- Should the Reflection Engine questions change?
- Is the Decision Engine useful?
- Is the Knowledge Graph growing healthily?

**Update**: ALL operating system documents

### Next Year Planning
- What's the vision for the year ahead?
- What are the 5 biggest priorities?
- What projects are ending?
- What projects are starting?
- What research should happen?

## Weekly Distribution of Work

Here's how different work types fit into the week:

### Strategy/Planning
- **When**: Monday morning (weekly), monthly/quarterly reviews
- **Owner**: You
- **Tools**: Decision Engine, Reflection Engine

### Creation/Deep Work
- **When**: Tuesday-Thursday afternoons (protected time)
- **Owner**: You
- **Tools**: PROMPT_LIBRARY, project specs, templates

### Collaboration/Communication
- **When**: Monday/Friday + throughout week in blocks
- **Owner**: You with collaborators
- **Tools**: Email, AI collaboration, meetings

### Research/Input
- **When**: Morning (30 min), + deeper on 1-2 projects/week
- **Owner**: You
- **Tools**: PROMPT_LIBRARY, research templates, sources

### Reflection/Learning
- **When**: Daily (30 min), weekly (60 min), monthly/quarterly/annual
- **Owner**: You
- **Tools**: Reflection Engine, journaling

### Administration/Maintenance
- **When**: Friday afternoon + scattered throughout
- **Owner**: You
- **Tools**: File organization, process updates, decision archiving

## Flexibility & Adaptation

This rhythm is a template, not a prison.

**Adjust for**:
- Season (intensity varies)
- Major projects (some weeks are creation-heavy)
- Reflection cycles (some weeks are learning-heavy)
- Collaborations (some weeks are communication-heavy)
- Travel or interruptions (compress where needed)

**The principle**: Scale your reflection with your activity.
- High-activity week → deeper reflection needed
- Low-activity week → lighter reflection sufficient
- Major project completion → deep reflection required
- Multiple small projects → weekly reflection sufficient

## Purpose

The Daily Workflow creates a **rhythm of compounding**.

Daily capture feeds weekly reflection.  
Weekly reflection feeds monthly insight.  
Monthly insight feeds quarterly strategy.  
Quarterly strategy feeds annual planning.

Together, they create a system where:
- Nothing is forgotten
- Everything compounds
- You become smarter over time
- The operating system itself improves
