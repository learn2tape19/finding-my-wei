## INTRODUCTION

A Campaign is a coordinated series of related assets published across multiple platforms over a specific period.

This taxonomy classifies all campaigns by:
- **Type** — What kind of intellectual work is being published
- **Scope** — How large is the campaign
- **Duration** — How long does it run
- **Audience** — Who is it for
- **Platform Distribution** — Where it appears

Use this taxonomy to:
- Plan publishing calendar
- Allocate resources
- Match content to audience
- Coordinate cross-platform messaging
- Measure campaign performance

## CAMPAIGN TYPES

All campaigns fit into one of eight types:

### Type 1: ORIGINAL RESEARCH CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Publishing original, previously unpublished research or findings

**Characteristics:**
- New intellectual work produced specifically for publication
- Original research, analysis, or clinical investigation
- Peer-reviewed or expert-reviewed before publishing
- Significant investment of time and expertise
- Core contribution to professional knowledge

**Examples:**
- Therapeutic Alliance framework (Project Atlas 01)
- Structural engineering concepts for Sidekick Air (Project Atlas 02)
- New clinical assessment protocol
- Research synthesis with original interpretation

**Typical scope:** Large (3-8 weeks production)  
**Typical duration:** 4-6 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** Website article, LinkedIn, email, newsletter  
**Typical asset count:** 8-12 assets (paper → article → carousel → video → newsletter → quotes)

**Success metrics:**
- Professional adoption and citation
- Speaking invitations
- Course/book sales
- Research collaborations
- Influence on field thinking

---

### Type 2: FRAMEWORK & METHODOLOGY CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Publishing proven frameworks, models, or methodologies for clinical practice

**Characteristics:**
- Organized thinking about specific clinical topic
- Practical applicability
- Tested in clinical practice
- Clear teaching structure
- Actionable for practitioners

**Examples:**
- How to assess therapeutic alliance in first session
- 5-step process for nervous system regulation
- Decision tree for common clinical situations
- Protocol for specific assessment or technique

**Typical scope:** Medium (2-3 weeks production)  
**Typical duration:** 3-4 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** Website article, Instagram carousel, LinkedIn, newsletter  
**Typical asset count:** 6-8 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Adoption by educators and clinicians
- Downloads and bookmarks
- Teaching requests
- Student applications

---

### Type 3: RESEARCH TRANSLATION CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Translating published research into practical clinical application

**Characteristics:**
- Based on existing published research
- Interpretation and application focus
- Bridges science and practice
- Honest about evidence limitations
- Clinical relevance emphasized

**Examples:**
- "What neuroscience says about manual therapy effectiveness"
- "The research on proprioceptive feedback"
- "What we know (and don't know) about fascia"
- Translation of recent research for practitioners

**Typical scope:** Small-Medium (1-2 weeks production)  
**Typical duration:** 2-3 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** LinkedIn article, website post, newsletter, quotes  
**Typical asset count:** 4-6 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement and shares
- Newsletter signups
- Research collaborations
- Credibility with researchers

---

### Type 4: CASE REFLECTION CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Learning from specific clinical cases and experiences

**Characteristics:**
- Grounded in real clinical work
- Teaching moment or insight
- Specific context acknowledged
- Uncertainty and questions included
- Honest about what worked and didn't

**Examples:**
- "What this patient taught me about nervous system regulation"
- "When my framework failed"
- "An unexpected clinical outcome"
- "Why I changed my assessment process"

**Typical scope:** Small (3-5 days production)  
**Typical duration:** 1-2 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** LinkedIn article, newsletter, Instagram story  
**Typical asset count:** 3-4 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement and comments
- Professional connections
- Teaching opportunities
- Community building

---

### Type 5: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Content supporting clinician growth and business development

**Characteristics:**
- Practical business, marketing, or practice management
- Professional development and career guidance
- Practice-building strategies
- Student and educator resources
- Entrepreneurship and independent practice

**Examples:**
- How to build a portable practice (relevant for Sidekick Air)
- Pricing and business model strategies
- Marketing for ethical practitioners
- Building professional credibility
- Course development and teaching

**Typical scope:** Small-Medium (1-2 weeks)  
**Typical duration:** 2-4 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** Website article, LinkedIn, newsletter, email course  
**Typical asset count:** 5-8 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Enrollment in related courses
- Product sales
- Consulting inquiries
- Community participation

---

### Type 6: EDUCATIONAL CONTENT CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Content created specifically for students and new practitioners

**Characteristics:**
- Foundational knowledge and concepts
- Clear, accessible teaching
- Structured learning path
- Assessment and reflection
- Future professional development

**Examples:**
- Nervous system fundamentals course
- Clinical reasoning frameworks for students
- Professional standards and ethics
- Career pathways and specialization
- Foundational anatomy and physiology

**Typical scope:** Large (2-4 weeks per module)  
**Typical duration:** Ongoing throughout year  
**Primary platforms:** Website course, email series, video  
**Typical asset count:** 10-15 per course module

**Success metrics:**
- Enrollment and completion
- Student feedback and ratings
- Job placement and career success
- Advanced course uptake
- Teaching and speaking invitations

---

### Type 7: PRODUCT LAUNCH CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Introducing new products, tools, or services

**Characteristics:**
- Announcement of new offering
- Education about product and use
- Value demonstration
- Testimonials and social proof
- Call-to-action for adoption

**Examples:**
- Sidekick Air table launch
- New online course release
- Certification program launch
- New book or publication release
- Tool or app introduction

**Typical scope:** Large (2-3 weeks pre-launch + 2-3 weeks post-launch)  
**Typical duration:** 4-6 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** All platforms coordinated  
**Typical asset count:** 15-20 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Sales/adoption rates
- Market share and position
- Media coverage
- Professional adoption
- Long-term market success

---

### Type 8: PROFESSIONAL COMMENTARY CAMPAIGNS

**Definition:** Commentary on industry trends, professional issues, or field conversations

**Characteristics:**
- Response to current professional discussions
- Timely but not trendy
- Thoughtful perspective
- Respect for alternative views
- Contribution to professional discourse

**Examples:**
- "The future of continuing education"
- "Professional standards and scope of practice"
- "Ethics in therapeutic practice"
- "Responses to industry changes"
- "Professional conversations that matter"

**Typical scope:** Small-Medium (3-5 days)  
**Typical duration:** 1-2 weeks publication cycle  
**Primary platforms:** LinkedIn, website article, newsletter  
**Typical asset count:** 3-5 assets

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement and conversation
- Professional recognition
- Media citations
- Speaking invitations
- Influence on professional thinking

## CAMPAIGN SCOPE CLASSIFICATION

### Scope 1: SPOTLIGHT CAMPAIGNS (Smallest)

**Definition:** Single post or quick release  
**Duration:** 1-3 days  
**Assets:** 1-2  
**Examples:** Quote with reflection, daily inspiration, quick tip

---

### Scope 2: WEEKLY CAMPAIGNS (Small)

**Definition:** Week-long coordinated content release  
**Duration:** 1 week  
**Assets:** 2-4  
**Examples:** Quick research translation, case reflection, professional commentary

---

### Scope 3: MONTHLY CAMPAIGNS (Medium)

**Definition:** Month-long publication and promotion cycle  
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks  
**Assets:** 5-8  
**Examples:** Framework publication, methodology release, research translation

---

### Scope 4: QUARTERLY CAMPAIGNS (Large)

**Definition:** Major intellectual release spanning 4-8 weeks  
**Duration:** 4-8 weeks  
**Assets:** 10-15  
**Examples:** Original research, course launch, comprehensive framework

---

### Scope 5: ANNUAL CAMPAIGNS (Largest)

**Definition:** Year-long initiatives  
**Duration:** Ongoing throughout year  
**Assets:** 20+  
**Examples:** Major certification program, book project, comprehensive curriculum

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Standard Campaign Components

Every campaign includes (at minimum):

**Research & Development (Variable timing)**
- Research and investigation
- Framework development
- Content creation
- Peer/expert review

**Publishing & Distribution (Fixed timing)**
1. **Week 1: Anchor Release** — Major piece (article, paper, video)
2. **Week 1-2: Multi-platform Assets** — Carousel, LinkedIn, quotes, snippets
3. **Week 2-3: Promotion & Engagement** — Social, email, newsletter
4. **Week 3-4: Deepening** — Email course, follow-up content, reflection
5. **Week 4+: Analytics & Learning** — Performance data, feedback, archive

**Measurement & Reflection (Week 4-5)**
- Analytics review
- Audience feedback
- Performance assessment
- Learning documentation
- Archive and repository update

## ASSET TYPES WITHIN CAMPAIGNS

### Core Asset: The Anchor

The anchor is the primary intellectual work:

- **Academic paper** (5000-10000 words)
- **Comprehensive article** (2000-3000 words)
- **Detailed framework** (illustrated guide, 20-30 pages)
- **Video lecture** (30-60 minutes)
- **Online course** (4-12 hours)
- **Book chapter** (5000-8000 words)

### Derivative Assets

Created from or supporting the anchor:

| Asset Type | Length | Platform | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn article** | 800-1200 words | LinkedIn | Professional network, thought leadership |
| **Instagram carousel** | 5-7 slides, text + visual | Instagram | Visual learning, shares |
| **Newsletter section** | 200-400 words | Email | Audience nurturing, curation |
| **Quote graphics** | 1-2 sentences + visual | All platforms | Daily inspiration, shareable |
| **Stories/snippets** | 1-3 tweets | Social | Daily engagement |
| **Email course** | 3-5 emails, 200-300 words each | Email | Deep engagement, conversion |
| **Video summary** | 3-5 minutes | YouTube, LinkedIn | Accessibility, different learning style |
| **Podcast episode** | 20-30 minutes | Podcast | Conversation, accessibility |
| **Webinar/live Q&A** | 60-90 minutes | Zoom | Interaction, community |
| **Infographic** | Visual only | Pinterest, Instagram, LinkedIn | Data visualization |
| **Case study** | 1000-1500 words | Website, LinkedIn | Practical application |
| **Worksheet/template** | Downloadable resource | Website | Practical application, lead generation |
| **Discussion guide** | 1-2 pages | Email, website | Group learning, teaching |

## CAMPAIGN EXAMPLES

### Example 1: THERAPEUTIC ALLIANCE Research Campaign (Project Atlas 01)

**Type:** Original Research Campaign  
**Scope:** Quarterly  
**Duration:** 8 weeks

**Anchor:** 
- "The Therapeutic Alliance in Manual Therapy: A Conceptual Review and Research Agenda" (Research paper, 8000 words)

**Derivative Assets:**
- LinkedIn article: "What the therapeutic alliance actually is (and why it matters)"
- Instagram carousel: 7 slides on nervous system safety in clinical relationships

- Video: 5-minute summary of research
- Podcast: 40-minute deep dive with researcher
- Newsletter feature: "Alliance and clinical outcomes"

- Discussion guide for educators
- Case studies (2-3) showing alliance in practice

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Anchor paper published on website, distributed to researchers
- Week 2: LinkedIn article, newsletter announcement, email course starts
- Week 3-4: Instagram carousel roll-out, video published, podcast airs
- Week 4: Email course completion, webinar Q&A
- Week 5: Analytics review, educator feedback, teaching requests

**Success Metrics:**
- Research citations and collaborations (primary)
- Speaking invitations and teaching opportunities
- Course adoption and enrollment
- Professional conversations and influence
- Foundation for future publications

## CAMPAIGN GOVERNANCE

### Campaign Approval Process

Before launching campaign:

1. **Conception** — Identify topic, develop concept
2. **Planning** — Complete campaign plan using template
3. **Editor-in-Chief Review** — Approval of concept and approach
4. **Asset Development** — Create anchor and derivative assets
5. **Review Process** — Editorial standards review (fact, message, visual, ethics)
6. **Final Approval** — Editor-in-Chief approval before launch
7. **Publication** — Coordinated release across platforms
8. **Monitoring** — Track performance, engagement, feedback
9. **Analytics Review** — Assessment and learning (week 4-5)
10. **Archive & Integration** — Content documented, data archived, learning integrated

### Campaign Capacity

**Maximum concurrent campaigns:**
- 1 large (quarterly) campaign
- 1-2 medium (monthly) campaigns
- 2-3 small (weekly) campaigns

This ensures quality and prevents overwhelm.
