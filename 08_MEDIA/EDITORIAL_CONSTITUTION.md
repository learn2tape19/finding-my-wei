## PREAMBLE

Finding My Wei creates wisdom through systematic investigation.

Project Atlas produces original work through rigorous research.

Publishing is the final stage of teaching.

This Constitution governs the editorial principles, standards, and processes that ensure every published work strengthens professional thinking and advances the field.

## PART I: FOUNDATIONAL PURPOSE

### Why We Publish

We publish to serve the profession, not ourselves.

**Specific purposes:**

1. **Advance Clinical Reasoning** — Help professionals think more clearly about complex clinical decisions
2. **Reduce Professional Isolation** — Create connections among thoughtful practitioners
3. **Bridge Theory and Practice** — Translate research into actionable clinical insight
4. **Preserve Institutional Knowledge** — Document the thinking of experienced clinicians before it's lost
5. **Challenge Conventional Thinking** — Ask difficult questions respectfully
6. **Build Durable Intellectual Asset** — Create work that remains useful for decades

### Why Finding My Wei Exists

Finding My Wei exists to capture, clarify, and share thinking that improves professional practice.

It is grounded in:

- **Curiosity** — Genuine interest in how things work
- **Humility** — Recognition of what we don't know
- **Rigor** — Commitment to evidence and careful reasoning
- **Generosity** — Willingness to teach what we've learned
- **Long-term Thinking** — Publishing for 10-year relevance, not 10-day metrics

### Responsibility to the Profession

We acknowledge responsibility to:

- **The practitioners we serve** — to provide honest, useful, accurate information
- **The patients they treat** — to improve clinical thinking through better education
- **The field itself** — to raise standards for professional discourse
- **Future clinicians** — to preserve and advance knowledge

## PART III: EDITORIAL VALUES

### Scientific Integrity

**Definition:** Commitment to evidence, acknowledgment of uncertainty, and intellectual honesty in all claims.

**Standards:**
- Every claim has evidence source
- Distinguish between: established science, emerging evidence, hypothesis, clinical observation
- Acknowledge study limitations and contradictions
- Never exaggerate effect sizes or clinical significance
- Cite competing research, not just supporting evidence

### Clinical Humility

**Definition:** Recognition that clinical reality is complex, individual patients vary, and our knowledge is incomplete.

**Standards:**
- Avoid absolute statements ("always," "never," "guarantees")
- Acknowledge context dependency ("works well in these situations")
- Welcome alternative approaches; don't demand agreement
- Respect clinical experience as valid evidence form
- Admit when we don't know or when evidence is mixed

### Practical Usefulness

**Definition:** Every published work should improve professional practice or thinking.

**Standards:**
- Content must be actionable or conceptually useful
- Explain "why" before "how" — clinical reasoning before technique
- Provide specific application examples
- Address obstacles to implementation
- Respect clinician time; avoid filler or unnecessary length

### Intellectual Honesty

**Definition:** Represent our own position accurately; acknowledge limitations of our thinking.

**Standards:**
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Distinguish between clinical experience and scientific evidence
- Acknowledge when we're advocating for a position vs. reporting facts
- Change our mind when evidence warrants it
- Publicly correct errors promptly

### Clarity Over Complexity

**Definition:** Express complex ideas simply without sacrificing accuracy.

**Standards:**
- Define technical terms the first time they appear
- Use metaphor and analogy to explain complex concepts
- Avoid jargon unless necessary; when necessary, explain
- Short paragraphs, clear structure, readable design
- Edit ruthlessly for clarity

### Generosity Over Self-Promotion

**Definition:** Publish to help the profession, not to build personal brand.

**Standards:**
- Teach freely what we've learned
- Acknowledge others' contributions and prior work
- Don't claim credit for obvious insights
- Share research and frameworks without gatekeeping
- Success is measured by professional impact, not personal metrics

### Long-Term Thinking

**Definition:** Publish for relevance in 10 years, not metrics in 10 days.

**Standards:**
- Avoid algorithm-chasing and trend-following
- Build on foundational principles that endure
- Create timeless content, not trendy content
- Measure success by lasting professional impact
- Preserve knowledge for future generations

## PART IV: EDITORIAL VOICE

### How Our Readers Should Feel

Our readers should experience:

- **Clarity** — "This author understands this deeply"
- **Respect** — "This author respects my intelligence and experience"
- **Hope** — "This is possible to understand and implement"
- **Challenged** — "I hadn't considered this perspective"
- **Included** — "This is written for someone like me"
- **Confidence** — "I can trust this information"

### How Educators Should Respond

Educators encountering our work should think:

- "This is worthy of teaching to my students"
- "This framework actually improves clinical reasoning"
- "This is evidence-based but practically relevant"
- "I can use this in my curriculum"
- "This reflects best practices in the field"

### How Skeptical Researchers Should Respond

Skeptical researchers should find:

- Methodologically sound reasoning
- Fair representation of evidence
- Honest acknowledgment of limitations
- Appropriate confidence levels matched to evidence
- Openness to scientific challenge and refinement

### How Experienced Clinicians Should Respond

Experienced clinicians should recognize:

- Deep understanding of clinical reality
- Respect for their knowledge and experience
- Integration with what they know works
- Intellectual sophistication that matches their own
- Invitation to continue learning, not to start from scratch

### How Students Should Respond

Students should feel:

- Access to professional-level thinking
- Permission to develop their own judgment
- Foundation to build clinical reasoning
- Inspiration for professional identity
- Respect for their future development

## PART VIII: EDITORIAL ETHICS

### Publishing Ethics

**Never:**
- Exaggerate evidence or manufacture certainty
- Publish primarily to chase algorithms or vanity metrics
- Publish outrage, anger, or inflammatory content
- Attack colleagues or competing approaches
- Publish without genuine value to offer
- Mislead about expertise or credentials
- Copy or plagiarize without attribution
- Publish confidential patient information
- Misrepresent research findings
- Create artificial controversy for attention

**Always:**
- Acknowledge what we don't know
- Cite sources and competing viewpoints
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Respect audience intelligence
- Publish to teach and improve practice
- Correct errors publicly and promptly
- Give credit to predecessors and collaborators
- Seek feedback before finalizing
- Review for bias and blind spots
- Remember we're representing the profession

### Retraction and Correction Policy

If published material is found to be inaccurate:

1. **Assess severity** — Is it a minor error or material misstatement?
2. **Research** — Determine what's actually correct
3. **Correct** — Publish correction or retraction prominently
4. **Explain** — Be transparent about what was wrong and why
5. **Learn** — Update processes to prevent recurrence

## PART IX: CAMPAIGN PHILOSOPHY

### One Idea, Many Assets

Every significant insight or research finding should be published across multiple formats:

```
CORE IDEA
(Research finding, clinical framework, or important insight)
    ↓
ORIGINAL PAPER
(Detailed academic publication, 5000+ words)
    ↓
ARTICLE
(Accessible version for professionals, 2000-3000 words)
    ↓
LINKEDIN POST
(Professional network, 800-1200 words, career-focused angle)
    ↓
INSTAGRAM CAROUSEL
(Visual learning, 5-7 slides, key points, shareable)
    ↓
STORIES & QUOTES
(Daily social posting, bite-sized wisdom, quotable insights)
    ↓
NEWSLETTER
(Email, curated weekly, connecting themes)
    ↓
LECTURE/PRESENTATION
(Live teaching, slides, speaker notes, Q&A)
    ↓
COURSE CONTENT
(Educational module, structured learning, assessments)
    ↓
FUTURE BOOK CHAPTER
(Comprehensive treatment, integrated with other chapters)
```

### Why This Philosophy Works

- **Audience Diversity:** Different audiences consume content on different platforms
- **Learning Styles:** Some learn through reading, others through visual, others through conversation
- **Leverage:** One research effort produces 10 publishable assets
- **Visibility:** Same idea reaching multiple audiences amplifies impact
- **Authority:** Consistent message across platforms builds credibility
- **Efficiency:** Templates and systems make asset creation faster with each iteration

### Asset Interdependence

Every asset links to related assets:
- Article links to paper for detailed evidence
- Social posts link to articles for depth
- Newsletter curates related articles
- Course modules reference papers
- Books integrate chapter topics

This creates an intellectual web where readers move deeper as needed.

## PART X: PUBLISHING RHYTHM

### Strategic Publishing Cycle

**Quarterly (Once per Season):**
- Major research release or original paper
- Comprehensive framework article (5000+ words)
- New educational course or certification
- Book excerpt or chapter release
- Industry analysis or state-of-field assessment

**Monthly:**
- Three to four article releases (2000-3000 words each)
- One major social campaign
- Newsletter (weekly, but curated content)
- Speaking or teaching engagement

**Weekly:**
- Newsletter with curated content (Tuesday)
- Two to three social campaigns (distributed across days)
- Engagement and responses to audience

**Daily:**
- Social stories and quotes (1-2 posts)
- Community engagement and responses
- Opportunities to add to newsletter

### Rationale

**Strategic publishing** sets research agenda and establishes authority  
**Monthly rhythm** maintains professional visibility  
**Weekly consistency** keeps community engaged  
**Daily presence** reflects active thinking without overwhelming

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|

- Absolute view counts (without context)
- Likes without engagement
- Follower growth from bots
- Viral outliers with no lasting impact
- Metrics that don't connect to mission

## PART XV: ANNUAL REVIEW & EVOLUTION

### Annual Editorial Review (January)

Every January, the Editorial Constitution is reviewed for:

- **Effectiveness:** Are we publishing according to values?
- **Audience feedback:** What's working? What's not?
- **Professional impact:** Are we advancing the field?
- **Personal alignment:** Does this still match our purpose?
- **Market changes:** Do we need to adapt to profession evolution?
- **System performance:** Are processes working efficiently?

### Evolution Process

If changes to the Constitution are needed:

1. **Document the issue** — What's not working?
2. **Propose amendment** — Specific change and rationale
3. **Test** — Pilot change on small scale
4. **Evaluate** — Does change improve outcomes?
5. **Adopt or reject** — Update Constitution or revert
