# Campaign 001: Conversation Map
## The Intellectual Journey of a Clinician Engaging with Clinical Reasoning Framework

**Purpose:** Map the reader's journey from first exposure to full adoption.  
**Principle:** Every asset should exist to answer the next question the reader naturally asks.  
**Delivery:** One diagram showing flow, friction points, and asset alignment.

---

# THE CONVERSATION FLOW

```
STAGE 1: INITIAL ENCOUNTER
├─ Channel: Instagram
├─ Trigger: Educational carousel appears in feed
├─ Reader State: Scrolling. Skeptical. Distracted.
├─ Carousel Does: Creates tension. Challenges assumption.
│  "What if I've been assessing wrong?"
├─ Reader Question: "Is this true?"
├─ Next Asset: LinkedIn Article
└─ Friction Point: "I don't have time for this."
   → Solve: Carousel is 10 slides. Takes 60 seconds.

STAGE 2: VALIDATION
├─ Channel: LinkedIn
├─ Trigger: Clinician clicks carousel post link
├─ Reader State: Intrigued. Wants quick validation.
├─ LinkedIn Article Does: Deepens one element. Shows research context.
│  (Focus: "Nervous system regulation as clinical mechanic")
├─ Reader Question: "Does research actually support this?"
├─ Next Asset: Website Article
└─ Friction Point: "This feels like hype."
   → Solve: LinkedIn article mentions research foundation, invites deeper reading.

STAGE 3: RESEARCH FOUNDATION
├─ Channel: Website
├─ Trigger: LinkedIn reader clicks through for full article
├─ Reader State: Serious. Wants evidence. Wants to understand mechanism.
├─ Website Article Does: Teaches all five elements. Provides research context.
│  (Full 633-word article)
├─ Reader Question: "What does this actually look like with a patient?"
├─ Next Asset: Video (60-second) OR Newsletter Feature
└─ Friction Point: "Theory is nice. Can I actually do this?"
   → Solve: Website article includes clinical examples. Video bridges to practice.

STAGE 4: PRACTICAL APPLICATION
├─ Channel: Email (Newsletter) OR Video (YouTube/LinkedIn)
├─ Trigger: Reader wants to see clinical application
├─ Reader State: Converting. Wants to try this tomorrow.
├─ Video/Newsletter Does: Shows brief clinical moment. Demonstrates concept in action.
│  (60-second video OR 300-word newsletter feature)
├─ Reader Question: "How would I teach this to my students/team?"
│           OR "What's the full picture here?"
├─ Next Asset: Book (The Tao of Clinical Touch) OR CE Course
└─ Friction Point: "I get it, but I need more structure."
   → Solve: Video ends with invitation to learning resources.

STAGE 5: DEEP LEARNING
├─ Channel: Email (CE Signup) OR Direct Purchase (Book)
├─ Trigger: Reader wants comprehensive framework
├─ Reader State: Committed. Ready to invest time/money.
├─ CE Course/Book Does: Teaches complete framework. Provides teaching structure.
│  (4-6 hour CE course OR book chapters)
├─ Reader Question: "How do I integrate this into my daily practice?"
│           OR "How do I teach this systematically?"
├─ Next Asset: Advanced CE (12+ hours) OR Specialized Training
└─ Friction Point: "I understand it, but implementation is hard."
   → Solve: Book includes clinical examples. CE course includes practice planning.

STAGE 6: TEACHING & SCALING
├─ Channel: Direct (Educator signup) OR Sales
├─ Trigger: Educator/clinician wants to teach others
├─ Reader State: Committed. Now scaling beyond themselves.
├─ Training Program Does: Teaches how to teach. Provides curriculum.
├─ Reader Question: "What's the research really saying? (Going deeper)"
│           OR "How do we measure this? How do we know it's working?"
├─ Next Asset: Paper 1 (Research article) OR Assessment Framework
└─ Friction Point: "I need evidence for my institution/patients."
   → Solve: Paper 1 provides peer-reviewed validation.

STAGE 7: PROFESSIONAL INTEGRATION
├─ Channel: Professional journals / Conferences / Education
├─ Trigger: Clinician/educator becomes advocate
├─ Reader State: Integrated into practice. Spreading to profession.
├─ Paper 1 Does: Establishes research foundation. Creates profession-wide conversation.
├─ Reader Question: "What's next? (What's the edge of this thinking?)"
└─ Next Asset: Advanced research OR follow-on papers
```

# FRICTION POINTS & SOLUTIONS

## Critical Friction Point 1: "This is just hype / nervous system pseudoscience"

**Where:** Between carousel and LinkedIn article  
**Why:** Nervous system language can sound trendy without research context  
**Solution:** LinkedIn article immediately anchors to 40+ years of therapeutic alliance research  
**Asset that solves:** LinkedIn Article (mentions Norcross, Horvath, research foundation)

---

## Critical Friction Point 2: "I understand the theory but can't see how to actually do this"

**Where:** Between website article and video/newsletter  
**Why:** Five elements are conceptual; clinical translation is missing  
**Solution:** Video shows 60-second clinical moment. Newsletter feature shows real scenario.  
**Asset that solves:** Video (60-second) + Newsletter Feature (300 words, practical angle)

---

## Critical Friction Point 3: "This requires me to change everything I do"

**Where:** Between video and CE signup  
**Why:** Some clinicians see the framework and feel overwhelmed  
**Solution:** Emphasize that alliance assessment is addition, not replacement. Show incremental integration.  
**Asset that solves:** CE Course intro (focuses on "start here, not everywhere")

---

## Critical Friction Point 4: "Where's the evidence? I need research to justify this to my director/board"

**Where:** Between CE course and professional adoption  
**Why:** Institutions want peer-reviewed validation  
**Solution:** Paper 1 provides journal publication with methodology, evidence, research gaps  
**Asset that solves:** Paper 1 (peer-reviewed research article)

# CONVERSATION PRINCIPLES

1. **Every asset answers the next natural question.** Not "we have a carousel, so let's make it," but "someone needs to understand the research, so here's LinkedIn."

2. **Friction points are expected.** Anticipate skepticism. Build the asset that solves it.

3. **Each asset assumes the reader has read the previous one.** LinkedIn assumes carousel context. Website assumes LinkedIn context. No redundancy.

4. **The conversation eventually leads to professional integration.** The endpoint is not "book purchase." It's adoption and teaching.

5. **Paper 1 is the lynchpin.** Until research is published, some friction cannot be fully resolved. Paper 1 moves skeptics to advocates.

---

# IMPLICATIONS FOR CONTENT CREATION

**DO NOT CREATE:**
- Asset that doesn't answer a friction point or natural next question
- Asset that repeats teaching from previous asset in the conversation
- Asset that assumes the reader is starting fresh (they're not—they've encountered us already)

**DO CREATE:**
- Asset that moves reader to next stage
- Asset that removes a specific friction point
- Asset that assumes growing knowledge and commitment

## The Advocacy Ladder

The journey from individual understanding to profession-wide movement:

```
RUNG 1: CURIOSITY
├─ Experience: "I've never thought about this"
├─ Asset: Instagram Carousel (creates tension)
├─ Reader feels: Intrigued, but skeptical
└─ Question: "Is this real?"

RUNG 2: UNDERSTANDING
├─ Experience: "The research shows this is true"
├─ Asset: LinkedIn Article + Website Article (validates)
├─ Reader feels: Convinced the idea has merit
└─ Question: "Can I actually do this?"

RUNG 3: EXPERIMENTATION
├─ Experience: "I tried this with a patient. It worked."
├─ Asset: Video (60-sec) + CE Course (teaches how-to)
├─ Reader feels: Confidence they can implement it
└─ Question: "Is this real in my practice?"

RUNG 4: CONFIDENCE
├─ Experience: "I've done this 10 times. Outcomes improved."
├─ Asset: CE Course + Assessment Tools (measure progress)
├─ Reader feels: Ownership of the framework
└─ Question: "Why doesn't everyone know this?"

RUNG 5: ADVOCACY
├─ Experience: "My colleagues noticed the change. They asked why."
├─ Asset: Paper 1 (peer-reviewed evidence) + Teaching Framework
├─ Reader feels: Credible enough to share with others
└─ Question: "How do I teach this?"

RUNG 6: TEACHING
├─ Experience: "I taught another clinician. They changed their practice."
├─ Asset: Advanced Training + Curriculum + Teaching Videos
├─ Reader feels: Professional authority and responsibility
└─ Question: "How do we scale this?"

RUNG 7: LEADERSHIP
├─ Experience: "I'm now training educators. The idea is spreading."
├─ Asset: Leadership Program + Conference Speaking Platform
├─ Reader feels: Part of a profession-wide movement
└─ Question: "What's the frontier of this thinking?"
```

---

## What Creates Movement at Each Rung

### From Curiosity to Understanding
**What causes the transition:**
- Credible research (not marketing hype)
- Peer validation (other clinicians seeing results)
- Clear mechanism (understanding WHY, not just WHAT)

**Assets that enable it:**
- LinkedIn Article (research grounding)
- Website Article (mechanism teaching)
- Paper 1 (peer-reviewed validation)

**The critical question:**
"Is this legitimate, or is it trend?"

---

### From Understanding to Experimentation
**What causes the transition:**
- Simple first step (not overwhelming)
- Clear success criteria (how to know if it worked)
- Safety net (teaching material if it goes wrong)

**Assets that enable it:**
- Video (shows clinical moment)
- Newsletter Feature (practical first step)
- CE Course Phase 1 (teaches basic implementation)

**The critical question:**
"Can I actually do this with my patients?"

---

### From Experimentation to Confidence
**What causes the transition:**
- Repeated success (doing it multiple times)
- Measurable outcomes (seeing the difference)
- Integration into workflow (it becomes normal, not novel)

**Assets that enable it:**
- CE Course (full framework teaching)
- Assessment Tools (measurement framework)
- Case Study Examples (seeing themselves in the stories)

**The critical question:**
"This is working for me. Why isn't everyone doing this?"

---

### From Confidence to Advocacy
**What causes the transition:**
- Professional credibility (research they can cite)
- Peer validation (other professionals endorsing it)
- Institutional acceptance (it's not fringe)

**Assets that enable it:**
- Paper 1 (peer-reviewed evidence)
- Speaking Invitations (being asked to teach)
- Professional Network (access to other practitioners)

**The critical question:**
"Am I credible enough to share this with my colleagues?"

---

### From Advocacy to Teaching
**What causes the transition:**
- Colleague request ("Will you teach me?")
- Teaching framework (how to explain it to others)
- Curriculum materials (structure for teaching)

**Assets that enable it:**
- Advanced Training Program (how to teach it)
- Educator Curriculum (lesson plans, guides)
- Teaching Videos (show, don't just tell)
- Discussion Guides (facilitate learning)

**The critical question:**
"How do I teach this so others understand it?"

---

### From Teaching to Leadership
**What causes the transition:**
- Repeated teaching success (students are changing)
- Invitation to bigger platforms (conferences, institutions)
- Shared responsibility (network of teachers)

**Assets that enable it:**
- Leadership Program (scaling teaching)
- Conference Speaking Opportunity (profession-wide reach)
- Teacher Network (community of practice)
- Advanced Research (frontier thinking)

**The critical question:**
"How do we make this the standard, not the exception?"
