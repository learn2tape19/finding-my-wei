# Campaign 001: Educational Carousel
## Clinical Reasoning and the Nervous System

**Format:** 10-slide Instagram carousel  
**Story Arc:** Tension → Problem → Shift → Application → Teaching → Reflection → Invitation  
**Word Count Per Slide:** 30-40 words max  
**Visual Direction:** Minimal design, white space, concept-supporting imagery

---

## SLIDE 1: TENSION

**Headline:** What if you've been assessing the wrong thing first?

**Body:**
You assess tissue. You find the restriction. You apply your technique.

It doesn't work.

Why?

Maybe it's not about the tissue.

---

## SLIDE 2: THE RECOGNIZABLE PROBLEM

**Headline:** Same patient. Same restriction. Same technique.

**Body:**
First visit: Patient is anxious. Guards. Nervous system in protection.
Your technique fails.

Second visit: Same patient. Same restriction. Safe. Trusting.
Your technique works.

What changed?

---

## SLIDE 3: THE SHIFT

**Headline:** Not the tissue. The nervous system.

**Body:**
Your patient's nervous system determines whether tissue can change.

A nervous system in protection: Nothing changes.

A nervous system feeling safe: Everything changes.

This isn't neuroscience theory.

This is clinical reasoning.

---

## SLIDE 4: WHY THIS CHANGES TREATMENT

**Headline:** You've been working backwards.

**Body:**
Old: Assess tissue → Apply technique → Hope nervous system cooperates

New: Assess nervous system → Build safety → Then technique works

One sequence: Frustration.
One sequence: Results.

---

## SLIDE 5: ELEMENT 1 — YOUR PRESENCE

**Headline:** Your calm is medicine.

**Body:**
Your regulated nervous system directly regulates theirs.

They don't need you to talk about safety.

They need to feel your calm.

That's the first element.

---

## SLIDE 6: ELEMENT 2 — UNDERSTANDING

**Headline:** They feel understood when you perceive them.

**Body:**
Notice their breathing shift. Slow your pace.

See tension appear. Ask before proceeding.

Remember what matters to them.

Attunement creates trust.

Trust opens nervous system.

---

## SLIDE 7: ELEMENT 3 — MEANING

**Headline:** Explanation shapes their nervous system response.

**Body:**
"Your tissue is tight" vs. "Your nervous system is learning it can relax."

Same hands.

Different meaning.

Different nervous system state.

Different outcome.

---

## SLIDE 8: ELEMENT 4 & 5

**Headline:** Touch teaches. Choice safety ensures it sticks.

**Body:**
Touch provides learning their nervous system cannot get from words.

But only if they feel choice throughout.

"Is this okay? Continue? Stop?"

Agency + embodied learning = change.

---

## SLIDE 9: REFLECTION

**Headline:** Tomorrow, ask yourself one question before treating.

**Body:**
"What nervous system am I walking into?"

Not: "What tissue is restricted?"

That question changes everything.

What will you notice?

---

## SLIDE 10: INVITATION

**Headline:** Notice alliance in your next patient.

**Body:**
Strong alliance: They relax. Trust deepens. Tissue responds.

Weak alliance: They guard. Technique fails.

Begin observing what you control.

You might be surprised what you discover.

---

## VISUAL DIRECTION

**Each slide should:**
- Use 40% white space minimum
- Feature one visual concept (not decoration)
- Support the text idea (not replace it)
- Be readable in motion (swiping speed)

**Suggested visuals:**
1. Two paths diverging (tension imagery)
2. Before/after nervous system states (visual contrast)
3. Nervous system as concept (not anatomy)
4. Timeline showing sequence reversal
5. Clinician presence as waves/calm
6. Eye contact moment (attunement)
7. Same hands, different meaning (optical illusion style)
8. Touch + choice together (integration)
9. Clinician at treatment door (reflective moment)
10. Open invitation (movement/forward)

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, clean, 32-40pt
- Body: Regular, 18-24pt
- High contrast on background
- Plenty of breathing room

---

## CAROUSEL TEST

**Pass criteria:**
- [ ] Would someone pause and re-read a slide?
- [ ] Does curiosity pull them to the next slide?
- [ ] Does someone remember one sentence the next day?
- [ ] Would they see their own practice differently?
- [ ] Does it feel like teaching, not selling?

**If all yes: Carousel complete**

---

## NOTES FOR DESIGN

This carousel teaches one core idea: nervous system state comes before technique.

It does not:
- Explain all five elements in detail
- Provide tools or protocols
- Sell anything
- Use marketing language

It does:
- Challenge an assumption
- Describe a recognizable problem
- Reveal a shift in thinking
- Invite observation
- Make one clinician stop and think

The invitation (Slide 10) should feel like: "I wonder what I'll notice tomorrow?"

Not like: "Click here to learn more."

Curiosity > Call to action.
