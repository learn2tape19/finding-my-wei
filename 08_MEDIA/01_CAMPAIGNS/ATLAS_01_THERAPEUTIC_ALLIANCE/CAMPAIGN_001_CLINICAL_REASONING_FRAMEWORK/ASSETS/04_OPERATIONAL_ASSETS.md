**Dimensions:** 1080x1080px (Instagram square)

**Design Elements:**
- White or light background (calming)
- Quote text: Center-aligned, 24-32pt
- Attribution: Bottom right, 14-18pt
- Optional: Subtle nervous system illustration (left side)
- Optional: Icon representing the quote theme

**Typography:**
- Primary font: Manrope (from Finding My Wei brand)
- Size: 28pt (quote text)
- Weight: 600-700 (semi-bold)
- Color: Deep blue (#1a365d) or charcoal (#2d3748)
- Leading: 1.4-1.6 line height

**Color Palette:**
- Background: White (#ffffff) or cream (#faf8f6)
- Text: Deep blue (#1a365d) or charcoal (#2d3748)
- Accent line: Teal (#0f8b8d) or sage green (#6b8e7f)
- Optional shadow: Soft gray (#e2e8f0)

## Adobe Express Recommendations

**For Quote Graphics:**
1. Start with blank 1080x1080px template
2. Add background color (white or light cream)
3. Add subtle accent line (top or side)
4. Add quote text center-aligned
5. Add attribution bottom right
6. Export as PNG

**For Carousel Slides:**
1. Create 1080x1350px template
2. Set background color for this slide
3. Add headline (44-56pt, Manrope, semi-bold)
4. Add body text (18-24pt, regular)
5. Add slide counter (bottom right, 12pt)
6. Optional: Add simple icon or illustration
7. Export and repeat for slides 2-10

**For Social Cards:**
1. Use template library or start from blank
2. Maintain consistent header/footer across batch
3. Customize central content for each platform

# DISCUSSION GUIDE

**Title:** Therapeutic Alliance in Manual Therapy: Discussion Guide  
**Audience:** Study groups, classroom, continuing education groups  
**Format:** 2-3 pages, printable  
**Duration:** 60-90 minute discussion session

---

## Pre-Reading Assignment

Ask participants to read:
- Website article: "Therapeutic Alliance in Manual Therapy: A Clinical Reasoning Framework"
- Time: 10-15 minutes

Or watch:
- 5-minute video summary (if using for classroom)
- Time: 5 minutes

---

## Opening Discussion (10 minutes)

**Prompt 1:** "Tell about a patient who improved remarkably fast. What was different about that patient or that session?"

*[Listen for mentions of: felt safe, trusted me, understood the plan, nervous system calm, etc.]*

**Prompt 2:** "Tell about a patient where treatment wasn't working despite good technique. What do you think was missing?"

*[Listen for: didn't trust me, nervous, didn't understand, guarded, etc.]*

---

## Core Concept Discussion (20 minutes)

**Question 1:** "The article claims alliance predicts outcomes better than technique. Does that match your experience?"

*[Allow discussion; note agreement/disagreement]*

**Question 2:** "Of the three components—safety, collaboration, attunement—which one is easiest for you? Which is hardest?"

*[Different practitioners will answer differently; this is valuable diversity]*

**Question 3:** "How does this framework change how you think about 'stuck' cases?"

*[Move from "patient isn't responding" to "alliance isn't sufficient"]*

---

## Mechanism Discussion (15 minutes)

**Question 1:** "What nervous system state does your presence create in your patient?"

*[Reflection question; honest sharing]*

**Question 2:** "How does the neurobiology described (vagal signaling, interoceptive integration, neuroplasticity window) match what you observe in your practice?"

*[Connect theory to observation]*

**Question 3:** "Permission at the tissue level—have you noticed this? Does tissue respond differently when patient feels safe vs. guarded?"

*[Clinical observation validation]*

---

## Application Discussion (20 minutes)

**Scenario 1:** "Patient walks in guarded and skeptical. How would you assess alliance? What would you do?"

*[Guide them through the assessment + building alliance]*

**Scenario 2:** "You're applying good technique but patient says 'nothing's changing.' Alliance seems fine. What's your next question?"

*[Troubleshoot using alliance framework]*

**Scenario 3:** "Student applying technique perfectly but patient isn't responding. What would you teach them about alliance?"

*[Teaching application]*

---

## Teaching Implications (15 minutes)

**Question 1:** "If alliance is the primary mechanism, what does curriculum need to teach?"

*[Self-regulation, nervous system literacy, collaborative skills, attunement]*

**Question 2:** "How would you evaluate whether a student has developed alliance skills?"

*[Assessment ideas]*

**Question 3:** "What would it look like to teach alliance more explicitly in your program?"

*[Practical implementation]*

---

## Closing Reflection (10 minutes)

**Question 1:** "What's one thing from this discussion you want to try in your practice this week?"

*[Specific, actionable commitment]*

**Question 2:** "What question did this raise that you want to investigate further?"

*[Curiosity and ongoing learning]*
