# Publication Success Criteria

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 9, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define what success means for each publication within the Knowledge Lifecycle

---

## Success is Institutional Mission Advancement

Every publication is evaluated on whether it advances Finding My Wei's mission: to develop and share rigorous clinical knowledge that strengthens the therapeutic professions.

Success is not measured by likes or viral reach. Success is measured by institutional contribution.

---

## Evaluation Framework

### Clarity Tier (Does the publication communicate effectively?)

**Hypothesis Clarity**
- Readers can articulate the central claim in their own words
- Evidence in comments shows readers understood the argument
- Follow-up questions indicate readers engaged with ideas, not just reacted emotionally
- *Success criterion:* ≥60% of reader comments show understanding of central claim

**Logic Coherence**
- Arguments are supported by evidence or examples
- Counter-arguments are acknowledged
- Logical gaps are minimal (measured by reader objections)
- *Success criterion:* ≤20% of reader feedback reveals logical inconsistencies

**Practical Utility**
- Examples are concrete and clinically applicable
- Readers report trying the suggestion with their own practice
- Follow-up comments show implementation success or useful failures
- *Success criterion:* ≥30% of reader engagement includes "I tried this" or "this matches my experience"

---

### Impact Tier (Does the publication generate institutional value?)

**Meaningful Discussion**
- Comments reflect engagement with ideas, not just emotional reactions
- Readers contribute expertise or alternative perspectives
- Conversation goes deeper than "agree/disagree"
- *Success criterion:* ≥40% of comments add new information or perspective

**Questions Raised**
- Readers ask follow-up questions (curiosity indicator)
- Questions reveal gaps in thinking or edge cases
- Questions are specific enough to guide future investigation
- *Success criterion:* ≥8 specific, answerable follow-up questions in comments

**Perspectives Contributed**
- Readers share their own clinical experience
- Readers identify exceptions or populations where thesis doesn't apply
- Readers connect article to adjacent domains
- *Success criterion:* ≥5 reader comments contribute new clinical insight

**Research Questions Generated**
- Audience feedback reveals measurement gaps
- Audience identifies populations needing study
- Audience challenges assumptions worth investigating
- *Success criterion:* ≥3 potential Project Atlas investigations identified from reader feedback

---

### Reach Tier (Does the publication reach relevant audiences?)

**Audience Alignment**
- Engagement comes from target audience (clinical professionals)
- Not primarily from general interest or casual browsers
- Audience segment matches publication intent
- *Success criterion:* ≥70% of engaged readers are target demographic (verified by comment content, audience segment data)

**Platform Effectiveness**
- Each platform generates appropriate engagement type
- LinkedIn generates professional discussion, Instagram generates visual engagement, etc.
- No single platform dominates unexpectedly
- *Success criterion:* Each platform generates ≥15% of total reach; no platform >60%

**Website Authority**
- Article ranks for relevant search terms within 30 days
- Article receives traffic from organic search (not just social)
- Article links to and from other institutional work
- *Success criterion:* ≥20% of article traffic from organic search within 60 days

---

### Institutional Tier (Does the publication strengthen the institution?)

**Knowledge Base Integration**
- Article links to ≥2 previous institutional articles
- Article is linked from follow-up articles
- Article becomes part of an emerging knowledge system, not isolated piece
- *Success criterion:* Article is referenced in ≥3 future articles within 6 months

**Educational Reusability**
- Article can be repurposed for Learn2Tape curriculum
- Article can support CE course development (The Tao)
- Article can inform speaking content
- *Success criterion:* Article is excerpted or adapted into ≥1 educational material within 6 months

**Brand Consistency**
- Article reflects Tao clinical philosophy (evidence-based, patient-centered, nervous-system-informed)
- Article voice is consistent with institutional tone
- Article avoids marketing language, maintains clinical precision
- *Success criterion:* Editorial review confirms 100% brand alignment

**Long-Term Value**
- Article will still be useful and relevant in 2+ years
- Article won't need major corrections or contradictions
- Article makes claims grounded in enduring principles, not temporary trends
- *Success criterion:* Article contains no time-specific claims; frameworks are principle-based

---

## Scoring System

Each criterion is scored 0–3:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | Not met |
| 1 | Partially met |
| 2 | Substantially met |
| 3 | Exceeded |

## Example: Thursday "Permission Question" Article

**Publication Date:** July 11, 2026  
**Measurement Window:** July 11–August 8, 2026 (4 weeks)

### Clarity Tier

| Logic Coherence | ≤20% report gaps | 12% | 3 | Few logical challenges; those raised were edge cases |
| Practical Utility | ≥30% "I tried this" | 34% | 3 | Multiple readers reported implementation |

**Clarity Score: 9/9**

### Impact Tier

| Criterion | Target | Actual | Score | Notes |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Meaningful Discussion | ≥40% comments add perspective | 44% | 3 | Strong reader expertise shared |

**Impact Score: 12/12**

### Reach Tier

| Criterion | Target | Actual | Score | Notes |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Audience Alignment | ≥70% target demographic | 76% | 3 | Strong manual therapist/bodyworker engagement |
| Platform Effectiveness | Each ≥15%, no >60% | LinkedIn 42%, Facebook 31%, Instagram 27% | 3 | Healthy distribution across platforms |
| Website Authority | ≥20% organic search in 60d | 24% | 3 | Ranking for "permission nervous system" |

**Reach Score: 9/9**

| Criterion | Target | Actual | Score | Notes |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------|-------|

## Annual Review

Once per year, evaluate:
- Which publications scored highest on Institutional Tier? (These became permanent assets)
- Which publications generated the most Project Atlas investigations?
- Which publications were most reused in educational materials?
- Which topics generated the most reader discussion?
- Did audience questions reveal emerging research directions?

These patterns guide the next year's investigation priorities.

---

## Principles

1. **Measure institutional mission, not social metrics.** Likes are feedback; learning is success.

2. **Expect variation.** Not every publication will score equally across all tiers. A technical deep-dive may score high on Impact but low on Reach. That's fine.

3. **Avoid gaming metrics.** The criteria exist to guide judgment, not to incentivize manipulation. If you find yourself designing publications to hit scores instead of serving the mission, revisit the criteria.

4. **Use scores to improve, not to rank.** Success criteria are diagnostic tools. They reveal what worked and what didn't. Use them to inform next decisions, not to create competition.

5. **Adjust over time.** After six months of publications, review these criteria. Do they measure what matters? Are they too strict or too lenient? Refine them based on real-world results.
