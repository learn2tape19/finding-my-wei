# Capabilities Layer Charter

## Authority
Established by Drew Freedman as the shared enterprise services layer.

## Purpose
This layer provides **shared capabilities** that serve all domains without belonging to any single domain.

Capabilities are horizontal services. Every domain depends on some or all of them.

## Scope

### Inclusion Criteria
- Marketing and brand infrastructure (serves all domains)
- Publishing workflow and standards (how we publish)
- Research methodology (how we investigate)
- Analytics and measurement (how we know impact)
- Operations and infrastructure (what we share)
- Quality assurance and review (how we validate)
- Knowledge synthesis (how research becomes frameworks)

### Exclusion Criteria
- Domain-specific implementations → go to 05_DOMAINS/[domain]/
- Permanent knowledge → goes to 03_INTELLECTUAL_ESTATE
- Operational meta-layer → goes to 01_OPERATING_SYSTEM
- Research investigations → go to 02_PROJECT_ATLAS
- Constitutional governance → goes to 00_CONSTITUTION

## Governance

**Authority:** Drew Freedman (strategy), domain directors (use)

**Service Governance:**
- Each capability has its own CHARTER.md
- Service director is responsible for standards
- Domains consume services, provide feedback
- Evolution happens through use, not theory

**Decision Speed:** As-needed (services are dynamic)

## Major Capabilities

### MARKETING/
Brand, messaging, campaigns, templates shared across all domains.
- Strategy and brand guidelines
- Campaign templates and tools
- Cross-domain campaign management
- Analytics and reporting

### PUBLISHING/
Workflow for turning work into books, papers, and published content.
- Publishing process and workflow
- Templates and standards
- ISBN and metadata management
- Distribution and sales

### RESEARCH/
Methodology and standards for investigations (used by PROJECT_ATLAS).
- Research protocols and frameworks
- Tools and infrastructure
- Peer review process
- Methodology standards

### ANALYTICS/
Measurement and reporting for impact, reach, and effectiveness.
- Dashboards and metrics
- Attribution and tracking
- Reporting and synthesis
- Data quality standards

### OPERATIONS/
Shared infrastructure, finance, legal, and systems.
- Finance and accounting
- Legal and compliance
- IT and systems
- HR and hiring (if applicable)

### QUALITY_ASSURANCE/
Validation and review processes for all work.
- Review standards
- Checklist and processes
- Escalation procedures
- Approval workflows

### SYNTHESIS/
Protocol for turning raw research into validated knowledge.
- Synthesis methodology
- Templates for frameworks
- Publication standards
- Quality criteria

## Lifecycle States
- **CREATION** → New capability is designed
- **REFINEMENT** → Capability is tested and refined through use
- **STABLE** → Capability is documented and widely used
- **ARCHIVE** → Capability is retired when no longer needed

## Relationships
- **Serves:** All domains (05_DOMAINS)
- **Supports:** Research (02_PROJECT_ATLAS)
- **Feeds:** Intellectual Estate (03_INTELLECTUAL_ESTATE)
- **Governed by:** Constitution (00_CONSTITUTION)
- **Uses:** Operating system (01_OPERATING_SYSTEM)

## Success Metrics
- Every domain uses at least 3 capabilities regularly
- Capabilities grow in response to domain needs, not theory
- Services are documented and discoverable
- Quality standards are maintained across domains
- New capabilities emerge organically from real needs
