## CE Course: Phase 1 (ACTIVE)

**Format**: 4-hour live course (single day or two 2-hour sessions)

**Learning Outcomes** (to be written NCBTMB-compliant):
- Understand permission as clinical foundation (not afterthought)
- Recognize nervous system signals and respond appropriately
- Integrate ethics into clinical decision-making
- Apply framework to own practice immediately

**Course Structure**:
- Block 1: Permission & Clinical Philosophy (60 min)
- Block 2: Nervous System Safety (60 min)
- Block 3: Ethics in Practice (60 min)
- Block 4: Integration & Q&A (60 min)

**Assessment**: Pre/post self-assessment + post-course quiz

**NCBTMB Submission**: Phase 1 spec due Q3 2026

## CE Course: Phase 2 (PLANNING)

**Format**: 12+ hour program (multiple sessions or extended workshop)

**Potential structure**:
- 4-hour Phase 1 (core framework)
- 4-hour Phase 2 (nervous system deep dive with demonstrations)
- 4-hour Phase 3 (ethics and decision-making case studies)
- Optional 4+ hour extensions (research review, specialized applications)

## Teaching Edition (FUTURE)

The book might evolve into a teaching edition with:
- Comprehension questions after each section
- Case studies with ethical dilemmas
- Nervous system reading exercises
- Reflection prompts for personal integration
- Instructor notes for group discussion

## Student Case Studies

As the course is taught, collect:
- How students applied concepts in their practice
- Outcomes they observed
- Questions that emerge
- Misconceptions that need clarifying
- Success stories and transformations

This becomes research data and teaching material.

## Purpose

Teaching infrastructure serves:
1. **Revenue** — CE courses are sustainable income stream
2. **Authority** — Teaching credibility positions book as serious work
3. **Feedback** — Students reveal what works and what needs clarification
4. **Refinement** — Teaching reveals gaps, future editions improve
5. **Community** — Students become ambassadors and future collaborators
