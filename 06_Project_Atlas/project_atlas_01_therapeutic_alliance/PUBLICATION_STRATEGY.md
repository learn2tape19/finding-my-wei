# Atlas 01: Publication Strategy
## Building Progressively Toward the Clinical Reasoning Architecture

**Status**: Strategic Direction Locked  
**Updated**: 2026-06-25

---

## The Strategic Insight

The Clinical Reasoning Architecture is the **synthesis and capstone** of Atlas 01, not its opening statement.

Publishing it first would invite the critique: "You're just organizing existing ideas."

Publishing it last — after establishing foundational papers — prevents that critique entirely. By then, the reader has followed the evidence and reasoning that makes the synthesis necessary and defensible.

---

## Publication Sequence

### Paper 1: Foundation
**Title**: *The Therapeutic Alliance in Manual Therapy: A Conceptual Review and Research Agenda*

**Purpose**: Establish that alliance matters in manual therapy and identify specific gaps in the literature

**Content**:
- Why alliance research in psychotherapy may not fully apply to brief manual therapy encounters
- What we know about alliance in hands-on modalities (PT, OT, massage, chiropractic)
- Evidence that alliance predicts manual therapy outcomes
- Specific research questions that remain unanswered
- Proposed research agenda for manual therapy alliance

**Audience**: Manual therapy researchers, clinicians, educators

**Publication Venue**: *Manual Therapy* or *Journal of Bodywork and Movement Therapies*

**Success Criteria**: 
- Establishes that this is a worthy investigation
- Shows that existing psychotherapy alliance research has gaps relevant to manual therapy
- Positions future papers as answers to the questions raised here

---

### Paper 2: Conceptual Development
**Title**: *Permission as a Clinical Construct: Toward an Operational Definition*

**Purpose**: Clarify what permission means as a nervous system + autonomy construct (distinguishing it from informed consent, shared decision-making, autonomy support)

**Content**:
- Historical definitions of permission, consent, autonomy in healthcare
- The gap: autonomy support exists, but nervous system basis isn't emphasized
- Nervous system perspective on permission (safety + choice)
- Operational definition: Permission = "The nervous system state in which a client feels simultaneously safe and agentic"
- How permission relates to alliance, expectations, and outcomes
- Research needed to validate permission as a distinct construct

**Audience**: Clinical educators, practicing manual therapists, patient safety researchers

**Publication Venue**: *Advances in Mind-Body Medicine* or *Patient Education and Counseling*

**Success Criteria**:
- Clarifies what permission means (not just a catchy word)
- Distinguishes it from existing concepts
- Prepares reader for why permission is central to the clinical reasoning architecture

---

### Paper 3: Integration & Architecture
**Title**: *Therapeutic Change Through Clinical Reasoning Architecture: An Integrated Framework for Manual Therapy Practice*

**Purpose**: Synthesize alliance research, permission concept, nervous system science into a coherent reasoning framework

**Content**:
- Integration of five evidence domains (nervous system, alliance, contextual effects, sensorimotor learning, autonomy)
- The five-phase reasoning architecture (Assess → Foundation → Enable Learning → Make Change Stick → Reflect)
- Seven guiding principles
- Clinical examples showing framework in action
- How this framework differs from existing clinical models
- Implications for practice and teaching

**Audience**: Manual therapy clinicians and educators

**Publication Venue**: *Manual Therapy* or *Journal of Bodywork and Movement Therapies*

**Success Criteria**:
- Reader understands the reasoning framework
- Framework is clearly connected to research (papers 1-2 have established this foundation)
- Reader can see how to use the framework in clinical practice

---

### Paper 4: Educational Application
**Title**: *Teaching Clinical Reasoning: An Educational Framework for Manual Therapy*

**Purpose**: Show how the clinical reasoning architecture becomes a teaching methodology

**Content**:
- How to teach the five phases and seven principles
- Curriculum design for CE programs
- Competency assessment (how to know if clinician understands the framework)
- Teaching examples and case studies
- Measurable learning outcomes
- Integration with existing manual therapy education models

**Audience**: Manual therapy educators, CE program directors

**Publication Venue**: *Manual Therapy Education* journal or education-focused publication

**Success Criteria**:
- Educators can use this to teach the framework
- Clear connection between framework understanding and clinician behavior change
- Sets stage for validation research

---

### Papers 5+: Validation & Refinement
**Title(s)**: *Clinical Reasoning in Manual Therapy: Evidence for Framework Effectiveness* (and related validation studies)

**Purpose**: Validate that the framework improves clinical reasoning, and that clinicians trained in the framework produce better patient outcomes

**Content**:
- Study design comparing clinicians trained in framework vs. usual care
- Measurement of nervous system markers (HRV, vagal tone) in clinical encounters
- Patient-reported outcomes (safety, permission, understanding, outcomes)
- Clinician reasoning quality assessment
- Modality comparison (does framework apply equally to different manual therapies?)

**Audience**: Researchers, clinicians, educators

**Publication Venue**: *Manual Therapy*, *Journal of Manipulative and Physiological Therapeutics*, or other peer-reviewed clinical research journals

**Success Criteria**:
- Framework-trained clinicians show measurable improvements in clinical reasoning
- Patients of framework-trained clinicians report better outcomes
- Framework effectiveness demonstrated across multiple modalities

## Strategic Advantages of This Sequence

### Logical Progression
Reader follows the evidence trail:
1. Alliance matters in manual therapy (Paper 1)
2. Permission is a key component (Paper 2)
3. These integrate into a reasoning framework (Paper 3)
4. The framework can be taught systematically (Paper 4)
5. Training produces better outcomes (Papers 5+)

### Defensibility
Each paper builds on previous ones. By Paper 3, the framework isn't "new ideas" — it's the synthesis of established research and concepts.

### Audience Development
- Paper 1 reaches researchers
- Paper 2 reaches clinicians and educators
- Paper 3 reaches the broad manual therapy community
- Paper 4 reaches educators specifically
- Papers 5+ reach the entire field (now with evidence)

### Prevents Premature Criticism
You can't publish a framework and claim originality until you've:
- Established that the question matters (Paper 1)
- Clarified the key concepts (Paper 2)
- Shown the integration is logical (Paper 3)
- Demonstrated it's teachable (Paper 4)
- Proven it works (Papers 5+)

## Integration with Atlas Constitution

This publication strategy exemplifies the Atlas uncertainty reduction process:

**Stage 1 (Paper 1)**: Research — establish what's known and unknown about alliance in manual therapy

**Stage 2 (Paper 2)**: Analysis & Synthesis — clarify key concepts (Permission)

**Stage 3 (Paper 3)**: Integration — show how concepts connect into reasoning framework

**Stage 4 (Paper 4)**: Creation — translate understanding into teaching

**Stage 5 (Papers 5+)**: Validation — test whether framework actually works

Each publication reduces uncertainty and prepares the next step.

## Broader Atlas Principle

This decision establishes a template for all future Atlas projects:

**Publication Strategy is a standard component of every Atlas investigation.**

Before research begins, define:
- What will be published?
- In what sequence?
- Why this sequence?
- What audience does each paper reach?
- How does each paper prepare for the next?

This prevents:
- Publishing too early (before foundation is established)
- Publishing without sufficient evidence
- Missing opportunities to build evidence progressively
- Claiming originality prematurely
