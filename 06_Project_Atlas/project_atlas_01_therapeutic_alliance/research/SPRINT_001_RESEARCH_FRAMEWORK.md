## Research Landscape Domains

Therapeutic alliance intersects multiple fields. Research must span:

### 1. Psychotherapy & Clinical Psychology
**Why it matters**: 85+ years of alliance research; established measurement scales; outcome correlations

**Key researchers**: Adam Horvath, Larry Beutler, James Prochaska, Jean-Pierre Bardet

**Landmark works**: 
- Norcross meta-analyses on therapy relationships and outcomes
- Horvath alliance measurement scales (Working Alliance Inventory)
- Lambert & Barley on therapeutic factors

**Unanswered questions**:
- Does alliance mechanism differ in somatic/manual therapy context?
- How does nervous system state affect perceived alliance?

---

### 2. Physical & Rehabilitation Medicine
**Why it matters**: Manual therapy context most relevant to Sidekick Air; patient outcomes measurable

**Key researchers**: Rosalyn Spence-Jones, Lucy Yardley, David Waddell

**Landmark works**:
- PT outcomes related to therapeutic relationship
- Patient satisfaction and functional outcomes
- Clinician communication and adherence

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific mechanisms of alliance in hands-on therapy?
- How does touch quality affect perceived safety?

---

### 3. Neuroscience & Autonomic Regulation
**Why it matters**: Biological basis for why alliance matters; nervous system mechanisms

**Key researchers**: Stephen Porges (polyvagal theory), Daniel Siegel (interpersonal neurobiology), James Doty

**Landmark works**:
- Polyvagal theory and vagal afferents
- Mirror neurons and empathy
- Nervous system co-regulation
- Interoception and body awareness

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific neurobiological pathways linking alliance to outcomes?
- How do vagal markers correlate with perceived alliance?
- Interoceptive accuracy and therapeutic trust?

---

### 4. Attachment & Relationship Science
**Why it matters**: Foundational theory for why relationships matter; individual differences

**Key researchers**: John Bowlby, Mary Ainsworth, Sue Johnson (EFT), Bessel van der Kolk

**Landmark works**:
- Attachment theory and secure base
- Adult attachment and therapeutic relationships
- Trauma and relationship repair
- Co-regulation in therapeutic context

**Unanswered questions**:
- How do attachment styles influence alliance formation?
- Can therapeutic alliance repair attachment injuries?

---

### 5. Somatic & Body-Based Therapies
**Why it matters**: Direct relevance to manual therapy; touch-specific mechanisms

**Key researchers**: Pat Ogden (Sensorimotor Psychotherapy), Peter Levine (Somatic Experiencing), Thomas Hanna

**Landmark works**:
- Sensorimotor psychotherapy and alliance
- Somatic markers hypothesis
- Touch safety and trauma
- Body-based communication

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific role of touch quality in therapeutic alliance?
- How does manual therapy touch differ from other forms?

---

### 6. Pain Science & Placebo/Contextual Effects
**Why it matters**: Mechanism by which alliance improves outcomes; expectation and meaning

**Key researchers**: Lorna Paul, Lene Juul Hindson, Ted Kaptchuk, Irving Kirsch

**Landmark works**:
- Context and placebo effects on pain
- Expectancy and clinical outcomes
- Provider behavior and placebo response
- Meaning-making in healing

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific mechanisms linking alliance to pain relief?
- How much of manual therapy outcome is alliance vs. mechanical?

---

### 7. Communication & Shared Decision Making
**Why it matters**: How alliance is built; what clinicians do that creates safety

**Key researchers**: Michael Barry, Glyn Elwyn (shared decision making), Enid Montaño-Loza

**Landmark works**:
- Patient-centered communication
- Shared decision-making frameworks
- Communication training outcomes
- Information exchange and understanding

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific communication behaviors that build alliance in manual therapy?
- How does explanation quality affect perceived competence?

---

### 8. Therapeutic Presence & Mindfulness
**Why it matters**: Clinician state as factor in alliance; attention and attunement

**Key researchers**: Betty Stawasz, Beatrice Hahn, Jon Kabat-Zinn

**Landmark works**:
- Presence and therapeutic effectiveness
- Mindfulness and clinical outcomes
- Clinician burnout and presence
- Attention and perception

**Unanswered questions**:
- How does clinician nervous system state affect client alliance perception?
- Can mindfulness training improve therapeutic presence?

---

### 9. Measurement & Assessment
**Why it matters**: How to operationalize and measure alliance; research methodology

**Key researchers**: Adam Horvath, Jean-Pierre Bardet, Ulrike Dinger

**Landmark works**:
- Alliance measurement scales (WAI, CALPAS, SWAI)
- Session-by-session measurement
- Patient versus clinician perception differences
- Predictive validity of alliance measures

**Unanswered questions**:
- Which alliance measure works best in manual therapy?
- Can real-time nervous system measures predict alliance?

---

### 10. Manual Therapy Specific Research
**Why it matters**: Direct application to clinical context; existing evidence base

**Key researchers**: Gilles Bronfort, Silvano Mior, Kerreen Reiger

**Landmark works**:
- Massage therapy and therapeutic relationship
- Physical therapy alliance and outcomes
- Chiropractic care and patient satisfaction
- Touch and healing outcomes

**Unanswered questions**:
- Specific mechanisms unique to manual therapy alliance?
- How does evidence from PT/OT/massage translate to broader manual therapy?

## Research Methodology

### Search Strategy
- PubMed (therapeutic alliance, clinical outcomes, manual therapy)
- PsycINFO (psychotherapy alliance, relationships)
- Google Scholar (broad scanning, emerging research)
- Hand searching landmark journals (Journal of Clinical Psychology, Pain, Manual Therapy)
- Citation tracking (follow references from key papers)

### Inclusion Criteria
- Peer-reviewed research
- Direct evidence on therapeutic alliance or related mechanisms
- Outcomes measured (not opinion/commentary)
- Relevance to manual therapy or somatic practice

### Quality Assessment
- Study design (RCT > observational > case study)
- Sample size and power
- Outcome measures validated
- Conflict of interest disclosure
- Generalizability to manual therapy

### Evidence Synthesis
- Separate by domain and method
- Identify converging evidence
- Note contradictions and explanations
- Assess quality and certainty of evidence
