## Primary Question

> **What engineering architecture will produce the safest, lightest, strongest, most manufacturable support stand for Sidekick Air, and what is the complete ecosystem of partners needed to bring it to production?**

---

## Research Hypothesis

Currently, we have:
- A validated prototype concept
- Provisional patent filed
- Clinical use validation

We hypothesize that:
1. Deployable structural systems from aerospace/military industries can be adapted for therapeutic use
2. Lightweight load-bearing is a solvable engineering problem (not novel, but proven in adjacent industries)
3. The right engineering partner exists — someone who sees the clinical/therapeutic opportunity, not just a furniture problem
4. Manufacturing at reasonable cost is achievable through design for manufacturing (DFM) principles
5. The knowledge system for finding the right partners is more valuable than any single partner discovered

This hypothesis will be tested through systematic research of engineering firms, adjacent industries, and prototype development.

---

## Mission

Design, engineer, document, and develop the **complete engineering and manufacturing ecosystem** for the Sidekick Air support stand.

**This is not a transactional project to find a manufacturer.**

This is a **question-driven research project** to build the **knowledge system that leads to the right engineering partners, prototype builders, suppliers, and production manufacturers**.

Every artifact generated becomes a permanent asset of the Finding My Wei Intellectual Estate.

The Sidekick Air stand is a **deployable structural system** (like military cots, aerospace platforms, emergency shelters).

Search like a structural engineer, not a furniture designer. Seek solutions to:
- Lightweight load-bearing structures
- Deployable mechanisms
- Manufacturing at scale

**Better knowledge produces better decisions. Better decisions attract better partners.**

## Research Standards

Every researched organization includes:

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Company | Name |
| Website | URL |
| Headquarters | Location |
| Industry | Primary focus |
| Core Competencies | What they do best |
| Relevant Experience | Projects similar to Sidekick Air |
| Engineering Capability | Structural, mechanical, industrial design |
| Prototype Capability | Can they build one? Speed? Cost? |
| Production Capability | Can they scale? Volume? Timeline? |
| Medical Experience | Do they understand medical/therapeutic equipment? |
| Why They Fit SAI | Specific strengths relative to our needs |
| Potential Concerns | Weaknesses, red flags, gaps |
| Recommended Next Step | How to engage them |
| Priority Score | 0–100 (using Evaluation Matrix) |
| Decision Maker | Name, title, contact method |

---

## Evaluation Matrix

How to score organizations:

| Category | Weight | Evaluation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Lightweight Structures | 20% | How proven is their expertise? |
| Folding Mechanisms | 20% | Have they solved deployment problems? |
| Engineering Capability | 15% | Design + analysis + manufacturing? |
| Prototype Capability | 15% | Can they build fast and iterate? |
| Manufacturing Capability | 10% | Can they scale volume? |
| Medical Experience | 10% | Do they understand therapeutic equipment? |
| Communication & Collaboration | 5% | Are they a good partner? |
| Geographic/Strategic | 5% | Location advantage? Market access? |

**Score each 0–100, multiply by weight, sum for total score.**

## Outreach Philosophy

Never ask: **"Can you build my stand?"**

Instead communicate: **"We are seeking an engineering partner to help develop the structural architecture of an innovative lightweight deployable platform for medical and therapeutic applications."**

The objective is to begin **technical conversations**, not request quotations.

This attracts engineering partners, not manufacturing vendors.

---

## Reflection Requirement

At the completion of every meaningful research session, answer:

1. **What did we learn?**
2. **What assumptions changed?**
3. **What new opportunities emerged?**
4. **What risks became clearer?**
5. **What should happen next?**
6. **What permanent knowledge should be preserved?**
