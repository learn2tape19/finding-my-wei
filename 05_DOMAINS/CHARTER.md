# Domains Layer Charter

## Authority
Established by Drew Freedman as the primary organizational layer of the IEMS.

## Purpose
This layer is where **intellectual work lives and is managed**.

Domains are business/knowledge areas with clear boundaries, governance, and authority. Every project belongs to a domain. No work should exist outside a domain.

## Scope

### Inclusion Criteria
- Domain-specific projects and products
- Work in progress, active, and completed
- Domain operations and decisions
- Domain-specific marketing and communications
- Domain personnel and relationships

### Exclusion Criteria
- Cross-domain research → goes to 02_PROJECT_ATLAS
- Shared services → go to 04_CAPABILITIES
- Permanent knowledge (when completed) → goes to 03_INTELLECTUAL_ESTATE
- Constitutional governance → goes to 00_CONSTITUTION
- Operational meta-layer → goes to 01_OPERATING_SYSTEM

## Governance

**Authority:** Domain director (Drew Freedman or delegated), guided by Constitutional principles

**Domain Governance Structure:**
- Each domain has its own CHARTER.md defining scope and authority
- Domain directors make decisions within their domains
- Cross-domain decisions go through governance process
- Constitutional layer is consulted for major changes

**Amendment Speed:** As-needed (domains are dynamic)

## Adding New Domains

Process for adding a new domain:
1. Business need is identified
2. Domain scope and authority are defined
3. CHARTER.md is created
4. Drew Freedman approves
5. Domain directory is created
6. Domain is added to this list

## Lifecycle States
- **CREATION** → Domain is being defined
- **REFINEMENT** → Domain is establishing operations and structure
- **STABLE** → Domain is operating with established processes
- **PUBLICATION** → Domain's work is being published/shared
- **ARCHIVE** → Domain is retired or historical

## Relationships
- **Uses services:** 04_CAPABILITIES (marketing, publishing, research, analytics, operations, quality, synthesis)
- **Participates in:** 02_PROJECT_ATLAS (cross-domain research)
- **Contributes to:** 03_INTELLECTUAL_ESTATE (completed work)
- **Governed by:** 00_CONSTITUTION
- **Operates within:** 01_OPERATING_SYSTEM

## Success Metrics
- Each domain has clear authority and governance
- Work flow through domains is predictable
- Domains can add new projects without chaos
- Knowledge moves from domains to Intellectual Estate
- Domains share infrastructure efficiently
- Future collaborators understand domain boundaries
