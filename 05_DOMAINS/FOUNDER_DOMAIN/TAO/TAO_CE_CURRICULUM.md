# Relationship to Domain

This curriculum shows how the intellectual domain in **TAO_DOMAIN.md** becomes education.

It does not replace the domain.

It answers: "How do we teach practitioners to think within the 7 Intellectual Pillars and contribute to answering the Governing Question?"

---

# Education Philosophy

Continuing education is not a product.

It is the educational expression of an intellectual domain.

Every course should:
- Emerge from documented intellectual work (TAO_DOMAIN.md)
- Reference underlying evidence and research
- Map to one or more Intellectual Pillars
- Address one or more Research Questions
- Identify unresolved questions
- Evolve as understanding improves
- Cultivate better thinkers, not merely better technicians

**Teaching exists to advance the domain. Not the reverse.**

---

# Curriculum Structure

## Five Levels

```
INTRODUCTORY    (4-6 hrs)      ← Framework & basics
       ↓
INTERMEDIATE    (8-10 hrs)     ← Applied to practice
       ↓
ADVANCED        (12-16 hrs)    ← Clinical mastery
       ↓
INSTRUCTOR      (20-24 hrs)    ← Teaching certification
       ↓
MASTER          (30+ hrs)      ← Leadership & vision
```

Each level is freestanding but sequencing enhances depth.

---

# LEVEL I: INTRODUCTORY

**Duration:** 4-6 hours  
**CEUs:** 4  
**Audience:** All practitioners  

**Pillars Addressed:** 1, 2, 3 (Alliance, Permission, Safety)  
**Research Questions:** What is permission? How does alliance work? Why does safety matter?  

### Learning Objectives
- Define permission as nervous system state
- Understand how therapeutic alliance works
- Recognize nervous system safety principles
- Identify when permission is present/absent

### Content
- Module 1: Permission as framework
- Module 2: Therapeutic alliance fundamentals
- Module 3: Nervous system basics
- Module 4: Integration into practice

**Assessment:** Attendance; optional reflection

---

# LEVEL II: INTERMEDIATE

**Duration:** 8-10 hours  
**CEUs:** 8  
**Prerequisites:** Level I or equivalent  
**Audience:** Practitioners ready to integrate framework  

**Pillars Addressed:** 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 (Alliance, Permission, Safety, Reasoning, Adaptation)  
**Research Questions:** How do I assess permission? When do I refer? How do I repair rupture?  

### Learning Objectives
- Apply permission assessment to clinical presentations
- Modify approach based on permission state
- Recognize and repair rupture
- Teach clients about nervous systems
- Identify appropriate referral points

### Content
- Module 1: Permission assessment protocols
- Module 2: Clinical reasoning through permission lens
- Module 3: Permission repair and alliance recovery
- Module 4: Teaching clients about their nervous systems
- Module 5: Integration with your practice

**Assessment:** Reflection paper (1-2 pages)

---

# LEVEL III: ADVANCED

**Duration:** 12-16 hours  
**CEUs:** 12-14  
**Prerequisites:** Level II or 50+ hours experience  
**Audience:** Experienced practitioners seeking mastery  

**Pillars Addressed:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 (All except 7)  
**Research Questions:** How do complex presentations differ? What reasoning works best? How do I measure outcomes?  

### Learning Objectives
- Conduct sophisticated permission and nervous system assessments
- Design individualized treatment through permission lens
- Work effectively with complex presentations
- Teach other practitioners advanced concepts
- Contribute to field through research or teaching

### Content
- Module 1: Advanced assessment
- Module 2: Complex presentations (trauma, pain, etc.)
- Module 3: Advanced clinical reasoning
- Module 4: Teaching others
- Module 5: Research and innovation

**Assessment:** Case consultation + reflection

---

# LEVEL IV: INSTRUCTOR CERTIFICATION

**Duration:** 20-24 hours over 6 months  
**Frequency:** Cohort-based  
**Prerequisites:** Level III + teaching experience  
**Audience:** Educators  

**Pillars Addressed:** 1, 2, 3, 5, 7 (Alliance, Permission, Safety, Reasoning, Stewardship)  
**Research Questions:** How do I teach others? What makes teaching effective? How do I create permission in learning?  

### Program Structure
- Phase 1: Mastery verification
- Phase 2: Teaching skills
- Phase 3: Solo teaching (co-teach Levels I & II)
- Phase 4: Advanced pedagogy
- Phase 5: Ongoing mentorship

### Obligations
- Maintain current clinical practice
- Attend 1 Level III workshop annually
- Participate in instructor community
- Maintain professional standards

---

# LEVEL V: MASTER INSTRUCTOR

**Duration:** 30+ hours over 12 months  
**Frequency:** Invitation-only  
**Prerequisites:** Instructor Certification + 2 years + demonstrated leadership  
**Audience:** Educator-leaders  

**Pillars Addressed:** All 7 pillars  
**Research Questions:** How does the field evolve? How do we train leaders? What moves a profession forward?  

### Program Focus
- Curriculum development and innovation
- Instructor training and leadership
- Research and scholarship contribution
- Field leadership and vision

# What This Curriculum Represents

Teaching is how the domain perpetuates and evolves.

Each course should create practitioners who:
- Think in terms of the 7 Intellectual Pillars
- Can discuss Permission as organizing principle
- Understand the Governing Question
- Can identify Research Questions their work addresses
- Remain open to evidence and evolution

The curriculum is not a technique course.

It is a pathway for practitioner transformation.
