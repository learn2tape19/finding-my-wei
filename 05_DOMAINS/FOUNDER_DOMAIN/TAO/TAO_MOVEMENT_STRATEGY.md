# Relationship to Domain

This strategy shows how the intellectual domain in **TAO_DOMAIN.md** becomes institutional change.

It does not replace the domain.

It answers: "How do we change what's considered normal practice in the field?"

---

# Movement Philosophy

A movement changes what's normal.

Not through sales, but through adoption.

When permission-based practice becomes standard:
- Schools teach it foundationally
- Professional organizations adopt it as standard
- Clients expect it
- Outcomes improve across the field
- Practitioner satisfaction increases
- Profession credibility strengthens

# Adoption by Stakeholder

## Educators (Highest Leverage)
- Identify and train early adopters
- Offer Level IV instructor certification
- Provide curriculum support
- Target: 50+ schools by year 5

## Professional Organizations
- Present evidence at conferences
- Propose competency standards
- Influence certification boards
- Target: NBCTMB competency inclusion by year 4

## Healthcare Systems
- Publish outcomes research
- Partner for outcomes measurement
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness
- Target: 3-5 healthcare system partnerships by year 5

## Practitioners
- Level I training access (online + live)
- Practitioner community building
- Career advancement pathways
- Target: 15,000+ trained by year 5

## Researchers
- Make findings accessible
- Invite collaboration
- Train research methods
- Target: Active research network by year 3

# What This Movement Strategy Represents

A movement is how ideas become normal.

It requires:
- Evidence that the approach works
- Access (easy to learn)
- Community (support networks)
- Leadership (visible advocates)
- Integration (institutional adoption)

The goal is not followers.

It is a field that thinks differently.
