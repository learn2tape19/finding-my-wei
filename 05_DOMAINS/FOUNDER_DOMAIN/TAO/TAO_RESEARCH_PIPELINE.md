# Research Priorities (2026-2036)

## PRIORITY 1: Permission as Measurable Construct (2026-2028)

**Pillar:** Permission (Pillar 2)  
**Research Questions:** Can permission be reliably assessed? What nervous system markers indicate it?  

### Study 1.1: Permission Assessment Scale Development
**Goal:** Develop validated instrument for permission assessment  
**Design:** Qualitative interviews (n=15-20) → Expert consensus → Pilot (n=50-100) → Validation (n=200-300)  
**Outcome:** Permission Assessment Scale (validated tool)  
**Publication:** "Development and Validation of a Permission Assessment Scale"  

### Study 1.2: Physiological Markers of Permission
**Goal:** Identify measurable nervous system indicators  
**Design:** Measure HRV, respiratory coherence, skin conductance during sessions with/without permission  
**Outcome:** Identified nervous system signature of permission  
**Publication:** "Physiological Markers of Permission in Manual Therapy"  

---

## PRIORITY 2: Alliance & Outcomes (2027-2029)

**Pillar:** Therapeutic Alliance (Pillar 1)  
**Research Questions:** Does alliance predict better outcomes? Can training improve effectiveness?  

### Study 2.1: Does Alliance Predict Outcomes?
**Goal:** Prospective cohort study of alliance and outcomes  
**Design:** Recruit clients (n=100-150); measure alliance + outcomes; regression analysis  
**Outcome:** Evidence that alliance predicts better outcomes than technique  
**Publication:** "Therapeutic Alliance as Predictor of Outcomes in Manual Therapy"  

### Study 2.2: Alliance Training Effects
**Goal:** Randomized controlled trial of alliance-focused training  
**Design:** Randomize practitioners (n=80-120); measure client outcomes; compare groups  
**Outcome:** Evidence that training works; validates curriculum  
**Publication:** "Randomized Trial of Alliance-Focused Training"  

---

## PRIORITY 3: Specific Populations (2027-2031)

**Pillar:** All pillars in application context  
**Research Questions:** How do permission-based principles apply to different populations?  

### Study 3.1: Permission in Trauma Survivors
**Design:** Case series (n=10-15); detailed documentation of permission-based approach  
**Outcome:** Understanding of trauma + permission relationship  
**Publication:** Case reports + clinical review

### Study 3.2: Permission in Chronic Pain
**Design:** Prospective cohort (n=50-80); pain + permission + function measured  
**Outcome:** Evidence linking permission to pain outcomes  
**Publication:** Case series + outcomes paper

### Study 3.3: Permission in Athletes
**Design:** Collaborate with athletic training program; track performance + recovery + permission  
**Outcome:** Evidence for athletic applications  
**Publication:** Specialized populations paper

---

## PRIORITY 4: Teaching & Learning (2028-2032)

**Pillar:** Stewardship (Pillar 7)  
**Research Questions:** Does permission-based teaching produce different practitioners?  

### Study 4.1: Teaching Style & Practitioner Outcomes
**Design:** Survey/interview practitioners (n=100+) on training received vs. current practice  
**Outcome:** Evidence linking teaching style to practice style  
**Publication:** "How Teachers Shape Practitioners"

### Study 4.2: What Makes Teaching Work
**Design:** Qualitative study of excellent permission-based teachers (n=5-10)  
**Outcome:** Teaching protocols and key elements  
**Publication:** "Teaching Permission-Based Practice"

---

## PRIORITY 5: Mechanism Studies (2029-2033)

**Pillar:** All pillars  
**Research Questions:** HOW does permission create healing? WHAT mechanisms operate?  

### Study 5.1: Permission → Outcomes Pathway
**Design:** Detailed video analysis of sessions (n=20-30); identify permission-building moments; track outcomes  
**Outcome:** Understanding of healing mechanism  
**Publication:** "Mechanisms of Permission-Based Healing"

### Study 5.2: Practitioner Nervous System & Outcomes
**Design:** Measure practitioner HRV + nervous system state; correlate with client outcomes  
**Outcome:** Evidence that practitioner regulation matters  
**Publication:** "Co-Regulation in Manual Therapy"

---

## PRIORITY 6: Implementation & Scale (2030-2034)

**Pillar:** Stewardship (Pillar 7)  
**Research Questions:** How does permission-based practice scale? What enables adoption?  

### Study 6.1: Cost-Effectiveness Analysis
**Design:** Track 500+ clients; measure costs vs. outcomes; compare across practitioner training backgrounds  
**Outcome:** Health economics evidence  
**Publication:** Cost-effectiveness paper

### Study 6.2: Implementation Science
**Design:** Document barriers and facilitators to regional scaling  
**Outcome:** Implementation protocols  
**Publication:** "Scaling Permission-Based Practice"
