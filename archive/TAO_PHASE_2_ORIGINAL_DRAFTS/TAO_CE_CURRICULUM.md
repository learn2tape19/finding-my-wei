# CE Philosophy

Continuing education in permission-based practice differs from traditional curriculum in a fundamental way:

**Traditional CE:** "Here is a technique. Here are the steps. Practice them."

**Permission-Based CE:** "Here is a framework. Here is how your nervous system works. Here is how to think about your client's nervous system. Here is how to teach others. Here is how to evolve."

Every program builds:
1. **Conceptual understanding** (what to think about)
2. **Nervous system awareness** (how to perceive)
3. **Clinical decision-making** (how to choose)
4. **Teaching capacity** (how to share with others)

# LEVEL STRUCTURE

## Overview
```
INTRODUCTORY       — Permission as framework (4-6 hours)
INTERMEDIATE       — Applied permission (8-10 hours)
ADVANCED           — Clinical reasoning through permission lens (12-16 hours)
INSTRUCTOR         — Teaching permission-based practice (20+ hours)
MASTER INSTRUCTOR  — Curriculum design and leader development (30+ hours)
RESEARCH TRACK     — Research methods and participation (varies)
```

Each level is freestanding (practitioners don't need prior level), but sequencing enhances depth.

# LEVEL I: INTRODUCTORY — The Permission Framework

**Duration:** 4-6 hours (live workshop or online module)  
**Frequency:** 6-8 times/year  
**Audience:** New practitioners, existing practitioners wanting foundation  
**Format:** Live instructor-led preferred; asynchronous online available  
**CEUs:** 4 (NCBTMB approved)

## Learning Objectives
Upon completion, practitioners will:
- Define permission as nervous system state (not permission granted by practitioner)
- Recognize how their clinical communication creates or destroys permission
- Understand how permission differs from consent
- Identify how nervous system regulation supports healing
- Recognize early signs that permission is absent
- Understand the role of therapeutic alliance in clinical work

## Content Structure

### Module 1: What Is Permission? (90 minutes)
- **Opening:** Real clinical story (from book prologue)
- **Concept:** Permission as nervous system state
- **Evidence:** Polyvagal theory, window of tolerance
- **Clinical Application:** How to recognize permission/lack thereof in session
- **Interactive:** Small-group discussion of clinical scenarios
- **Reflection:** Personal experience with permission/violation

### Module 2: Permission and Therapeutic Alliance (90 minutes)
- **Concept:** Alliance as mechanism (not side effect)
- **Research:** Why alliance > technique (meta-analyses)
- **Evidence:** Neurobiology of alliance, attachment
- **Clinical Application:** Building alliance in time-constrained session
- **Interactive:** Partner observation exercise (how presence changes)
- **Video:** Demonstrated session showing permission creation

### Module 3: Nervous System Basics for Practitioners (90 minutes)
- **Concept:** Body's primary agenda is safety
- **Framework:** Window of tolerance, regulation vs. dysregulation
- **Evidence:** How touch affects nervous system
- **Clinical Observation:** Recognizing dysregulation signs
- **Assessment:** Simple nervous system screening
- **Interactive:** Practice nervous system assessment

### Module 4: Teaching What We Know (60 minutes)
- **Reflection:** How were you taught?
- **Principle:** Practitioners learn based on how they're taught
- **Application:** Permission-based teaching
- **Vision:** What permission-based practice could change
- **Closing:** Integration and next steps

## Certification
- Attendance (no test required for Level I)
- CEU credit granted upon completion

# LEVEL II: INTERMEDIATE — Applied Permission

**Duration:** 8-10 hours (multi-day workshop or 4 online sessions)  
**Frequency:** 4 times/year  
**Prerequisites:** Level I or equivalent experience  
**Audience:** Practitioners ready to integrate framework into practice  
**Format:** Hybrid (2-3 days live preferred; online option available)  
**CEUs:** 8 (NCBTMB approved)

## Learning Objectives
Upon completion, practitioners will:
- Apply permission assessment to actual client presentations
- Modify technique based on permission state
- Repair permission after violation or rupture
- Teach clients about nervous system regulation
- Recognize when to refer based on dysregulation patterns
- Design sessions through permission lens (not technique first)

## Content Structure

### Module 1: Permission Assessment (2 hours)
- **Framework:** What to assess and how
- **Practices:** Interview protocols for permission
- **Observation:** Reading permission in posture, breath, tone
- **Interview Skills:** How to ask about permission safely
- **Case Studies:** Real examples of assessment
- **Skill Practice:** Observe partners; receive observation
- **Reflection:** Your assessment patterns

### Module 2: Clinical Reasoning Through Permission Lens (2.5 hours)
- **Framework:** Permission-first reasoning
- **Cases:** How permission state changes diagnosis
- **Red Flags:** When dysregulation signals need for referral
- **Decision Points:** Technique selection based on permission
- **Non-Imposition:** Offering possibility rather than fixing
- **Skill Practice:** Case reasoning using framework
- **Reflection:** Your current reasoning patterns

### Module 3: Permission Repair and Alliance Recovery (2 hours)
- **Reality:** Violations happen (accidental, perceived, actual)
- **Framework:** Stages of repair
- **Approaches:** How to address permission violation
- **Communication:** What to say when permission is broken
- **Neurobiology:** How nervous systems repair alliance
- **Case Studies:** Real examples
- **Skill Practice:** Rehearse difficult conversations

### Module 4: Teaching Clients About Their Own Nervous System (1.5 hours)
- **Principle:** Clients can learn nervous system basics
- **Benefit:** Informed clients have better outcomes
- **Language:** How to explain without jargon
- **Timing:** When to teach vs. when to work
- **Handouts:** Language and concepts clients can use
- **Skill Practice:** Explain a concept; receive feedback

### Module 5: Integration and Your Practice (1 hour)
- **Reflection:** What's already in your practice?
- **Barriers:** What blocks permission-first approach?
- **Planning:** One change for this month
- **Community:** Continuing support
- **Closing:** Celebration and next steps

## Certification
- Attendance
- Short reflection paper (1-2 pages): "How does permission appear in my practice now?"
- CEU credit granted upon completion

# LEVEL III: ADVANCED — Clinical Mastery

**Duration:** 12-16 hours (multi-day intensive or 6-8 online sessions)  
**Frequency:** 2 times/year  
**Prerequisites:** Level II (or equivalent 50+ hours practice experience)  
**Audience:** Experienced practitioners seeking mastery  
**Format:** Live intensive preferred (3-4 days); online available  
**CEUs:** 12-14 (NCBTMB approved)

## Learning Objectives
Upon completion, practitioners will:
- Conduct sophisticated permission and nervous system assessments
- Design personalized treatment plans through permission lens
- Work effectively with complex presentations (trauma, chronic pain, etc.)
- Teach other practitioners advanced concepts
- Conduct peer consultation on difficult cases
- Contribute to field through research or teaching

## Content Structure

### Module 1: Advanced Assessment (2.5 hours)
- **Deeper Framework:** Neurobiology of assessment
- **Polyvagal Deep Dive:** Vagal circuits and clinical application
- **Trauma Lens:** How trauma affects permission
- **Interoception:** Teaching clients nervous system awareness
- **Skill Practice:** Complex case assessment
- **Consultation:** Peer case review

### Module 2: Complex Presentations (3 hours)
- **Trauma & Permission:** How history changes approach
- **Chronic Pain:** Pain science through permission lens
- **Medical Complexity:** Working with multisystem clients
- **Relationship Trauma:** Trust deficits and alliance building
- **Grief and Loss:** Supporting regulation in emotional states
- **Case Studies:** Video examples of complex work
- **Skill Practice:** Case reasoning with complexity

### Module 3: Advanced Clinical Reasoning (2.5 hours)
- **Diagnostic Thinking:** Permission-first differential assessment
- **Pattern Recognition:** Clinical pattern libraries
- **Red Flags:** Advanced recognition of concerning presentations
- **Referral Criteria:** When and where to refer
- **Collaboration:** Working with other providers
- **Documentation:** Recording permission-based reasoning
- **Skill Practice:** Case consultation with experts

### Module 4: Teaching Others (2 hours)
- **Pedagogy:** How adults learn complex concepts
- **Demonstration:** Teaching through experience
- **Feedback:** How to help learners grow
- **Common Misconceptions:** Addressing permission confusion
- **Your Teaching Voice:** Finding your authentic teaching style
- **Skill Practice:** Teach a concept; receive peer feedback

### Module 5: Research and Innovation (2 hours)
- **Current Research:** What's being studied
- **Research Opportunities:** Questions that matter
- **Becoming a Researcher:** How practitioners contribute
- **Case Reports:** Writing clinical stories
- **Contribution:** Different ways to advance field

# LEVEL IV: INSTRUCTOR CERTIFICATION

**Duration:** 20-24 hours over 6 months (hybrid)  
**Frequency:** Cohort-based (1-2 cohorts/year)  
**Prerequisites:** Level III + 100+ hours teaching experience OR equivalent practitioner status  
**Audience:** Educators wanting to teach permission-based framework  
**Format:** Intensive weekends + online mentorship  
**Outcome:** Authorized to teach Levels I & II

## Learning Objectives
Upon completion, instructors will:
- Design and deliver Level I workshops independently
- Design and deliver Level II workshops independently
- Train new instructors in their region
- Contribute to curriculum development
- Maintain current research and clinical practice

## Program Structure

### Phase 1: Mastery Verification (2 days)
- Demonstrate Level III competencies
- Conduct assessment on volunteer clients
- Receive expert observation and feedback
- Document clinical practice standards

### Phase 2: Teaching Skills (3 days)
- Curriculum design fundamentals
- How adults learn complex material
- Demonstration and practice teaching
- Managing difficult group dynamics
- Assessment and feedback practices
- Creating psychologically safe learning environments
- Using video and real-world material

### Phase 3: Solo Teaching (6 weeks)
- Co-teach Level I with master instructor
- Co-teach Level II with master instructor
- Receive detailed feedback
- Refine teaching approach
- Build your instructor presence

### Phase 4: Advanced Pedagogy (2 days)
- Teaching different learning styles
- Online delivery for distance learners
- Building instructor communities
- Certification assessment standards
- Research and evidence integration
- Your contribution to field

### Phase 5: Mentorship & Oversight (ongoing)
- Ongoing consultation (first 2 years)
- Annual renewal (attend 1 advanced workshop)
- Curriculum updates and evolution
- Community of practice participation

## Certification Requirements
- Successful completion of all phases
- Teaching observation (both Level I and II)
- Passing written and practical assessments
- Commitment to annual professional development
- Agreement to maintain teaching standards

## Ongoing Obligations
- Maintain current clinical practice (minimum 4 hours/month)
- Attend 1 Level III advanced workshop/year
- Participate in instructor community of practice
- Maintain professional liability insurance
- Agree to regular curriculum updates

# ONLINE DELIVERY MODEL

All levels available online through:
- **Asynchronous modules:** Video + reading + reflection
- **Live discussion groups:** Biweekly case consultation (optional)
- **Practice coaching:** Individual or small group supervision
- **Virtual skill labs:** Recorded demonstrations + peer observation

## Online Advantages
- Accessible to remote practitioners
- Self-paced learning (asynchronous option)
- Affordable (lower cost than travel)
- Easier to repeat/refresh material

## Online Challenges
- Less hands-on skill development
- Weaker community building
- Harder to assess readiness

**Recommendation:** 
- Level I: Works well online
- Level II: Hybrid preferred (at least 2 days in-person)
- Level III+: Live preferred

# What This Curriculum Represents

This is not a traditional technique course with modules and quizzes.

It is a **pathway for practitioner transformation**.

Every level builds from the premise that how practitioners are taught determines what kind of practitioners they become. Permission-based teaching creates permission-based practitioners.

The curriculum is designed so that someone moving through it doesn't just learn *about* permission and alliance.

They experience it.

They feel it.

They practice it.

They teach it.

And years later, they're still deepening their understanding.
