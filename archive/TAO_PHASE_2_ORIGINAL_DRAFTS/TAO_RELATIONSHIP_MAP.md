Intellectual work is not solitary.

Every significant idea is shaped by conversations, challenges, collaboration, and trust.

The permission-based approach emerged through decades of relationships with practitioners, students, teachers, researchers, and patients.

This map honors those relationships and identifies who continues this work forward.

**Principle:** If you use someone's thinking, cite it. If you build on their foundation, acknowledge it. If you collaborate, partner authentically.

## Authenticity
- Genuine collaboration (not transactional)

- We contribute to others' work
- They contribute to ours
- Growth is mutual
- Success is shared

## Attribution
- Cite collaborators' thinking
- Acknowledge contributions
- Public recognition
- Shared authorship

## Long-Term Thinking
- Build relationships over years
- Trust takes time
- Commitment beyond projects
- Supporting others' growth
