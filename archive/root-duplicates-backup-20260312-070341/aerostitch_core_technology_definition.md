# AeroStitch Core Technology Definition
Author: Drew Freedman  
Date: March 2026

## Purpose

This document defines AeroStitch Core Technology, the structural engineering system developed by StitchCore for rigid inflatable human support platforms.

AeroStitch Core Technology represents an evolution of traditional drop-stitch inflatable construction adapted specifically for structural support applications involving the human body.

The technology enables lightweight inflatable structures to achieve high rigidity and load-bearing performance when pressurized.

---

# Technology Overview

AeroStitch Core Technology is a structural system built around high-pressure drop-stitch textile construction.

The system consists of two outer textile layers connected internally by thousands of vertical structural threads.

When the structure is pressurized, these internal threads maintain a fixed distance between the outer layers, creating a rigid load-bearing panel.

This construction allows inflatable structures to function similarly to rigid boards while remaining lightweight and collapsible.

---

# Core Structural Components

AeroStitch Core Technology is defined by the integration of several structural elements.

These include:

high-strength woven drop-stitch internal thread matrix  
TPU-coated textile outer layers  
RF or heat welded seam construction  
high-pressure inflation architecture  
reinforced accessory interface integration  

These components work together to create a rigid inflatable structural panel.

---

# Pressure Stabilization

AeroStitch Core structures achieve rigidity through controlled internal pressurization.

Typical operating pressure range:

10–15 PSI

At this pressure range, the internal thread matrix prevents the outer textile layers from separating, creating a structurally stable surface capable of supporting significant loads.

This system enables inflatable platforms to function as structural support surfaces.

---

# Load Bearing Performance

AeroStitch Core Technology is designed to support dynamic human loading conditions.

These conditions include:

body weight distribution  
downward therapeutic force  
shifting practitioner leverage  
dynamic treatment movements  

The structural system must maintain rigidity and stability during these conditions.

---

# Structural Thickness

Typical AeroStitch Core structural thickness ranges between:

4 inches to 6 inches

This thickness allows sufficient internal thread length to provide structural rigidity while maintaining portability.

The Sidekick Air platform uses a 4 inch structural thickness.

---

# Material System

Preferred materials include:

TPU-coated woven drop-stitch textile

Alternative material systems may include:

PVC-coated drop-stitch textile

TPU materials provide several advantages including:

reduced weight  
improved durability  
enhanced resistance to environmental degradation  

Material selection may vary depending on manufacturing partners and application requirements.

---

# Modular Interface Integration

AeroStitch Core structures incorporate reinforced interface zones for accessory attachment.

These interfaces may include:

mushroom-style fastener systems such as Dual Lock  
bonded attachment patches  
reinforced D-ring anchor points  

These features allow inflatable platforms to support modular accessory systems.

---

# Manufacturing Methods

Typical manufacturing methods include:

RF welding  
heat welding  
adhesive bonding of accessory patches  

Manufacturing partners must have experience producing high-pressure inflatable structures.

---

# Applications

AeroStitch Core Technology enables a range of inflatable structural products.

These may include:

portable therapy platforms  
sports recovery platforms  
emergency rescue stretchers  
medical transport platforms  
field treatment surfaces  
consumer recovery systems  

The Sidekick Air therapy platform represents the first commercial application of this technology.

---

# Relationship to StitchCore

AeroStitch Core Technology is the proprietary structural technology developed by StitchCore.

All StitchCore products are designed to be built upon the AeroStitch Core structural platform.

---

# Summary

AeroStitch Core Technology transforms inflatable textile structures into rigid, load-bearing platforms capable of supporting human activity.

By combining high-pressure drop-stitch construction with reinforced interface systems and modular architecture, AeroStitch Core provides the structural foundation for the StitchCore product ecosystem.
