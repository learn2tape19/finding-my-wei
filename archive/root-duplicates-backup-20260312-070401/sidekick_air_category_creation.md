# Sidekick Air Category Creation Narrative
Author: Drew Freedman
Date: March 2026

Purpose

This document explains why Sidekick Air represents the creation of a new equipment category rather than simply another massage table.

The goal is to communicate the strategic vision behind the product to investors, manufacturers, partners, and industry leaders.

---

# The Problem with Traditional Therapy Tables

For more than fifty years, the basic architecture of therapy tables has remained largely unchanged.

Conventional massage tables are:

Heavy  
Bulky  
Difficult to transport  
Slow to deploy  

Even so-called “portable” tables typically weigh between 28 and 35 pounds and require complex folding frames.

For mobile therapists, traveling sports therapists, and event recovery teams, this creates a significant logistical burden.

Many professionals simply avoid bringing tables into environments where they could otherwise deliver treatment.

The result is a large gap between where therapy could happen and where it actually happens.

---

# The Insight

The core insight behind Sidekick Air is simple:

A rigid inflatable platform can replace the traditional wooden or aluminum therapy table structure.

High-pressure drop-stitch technology, originally developed for marine and aerospace applications, allows inflatable surfaces to become extremely rigid when pressurized.

This technology is already used in:

Stand-up paddleboards  
Inflatable rescue platforms  
Military inflatable structures  
High-performance inflatable boats  

However, it has never been fully applied to professional therapy platforms.

Sidekick Air introduces this material system into the world of manual therapy.

---

# The Breakthrough

By combining high-pressure drop-stitch construction with a lightweight modular support system, Sidekick Air achieves three breakthroughs simultaneously:

Professional treatment stability

The inflated platform becomes rigid enough to support professional bodywork.

Radical portability

The entire system can be transported in a compact travel configuration.

Rapid deployment

Setup time is dramatically reduced compared to traditional folding tables.

The result is a therapy platform that can travel anywhere a therapist goes.

---

# A New Category

Sidekick Air represents the beginning of a new equipment category.

Rigid Inflatable Human Support Platforms

This category includes structures designed to support the human body while providing high portability and rapid deployment.

Potential applications include:

Therapy platforms  
Sports recovery systems  
Emergency stretchers  
Medical transport surfaces  
Portable rehabilitation systems  

Sidekick Air is the first product in this category.

---

# The StitchCore Platform

Sidekick Air exists within a broader innovation platform called StitchCore.

StitchCore refers to the structural technology and design architecture behind modular inflatable support systems.

The platform approach allows multiple products to share common engineering principles.

These products may include:

Portable therapy tables  
Emergency stretchers  
Medical gurneys  
Field treatment platforms  
Consumer recovery systems

By building around a platform architecture rather than a single product, the technology can scale across multiple industries.

# Market Opportunity

Several large markets intersect with this new category.

Mobile therapy professionals

Massage therapists, sports therapists, and manual therapy practitioners who travel to clients.

Sports medicine

Professional and amateur athletic environments that require portable treatment equipment.

Event recovery environments

Marathons, competitions, and endurance events where portable treatment stations are essential.

Emergency and rescue services

Lightweight portable stretchers for difficult environments.

Consumer wellness

Home users seeking portable recovery and therapy solutions.

These combined markets represent a substantial opportunity for category creation.

# Strategic Visibility

High-visibility events provide powerful opportunities for the platform.

One example is the Los Angeles Olympic Games in 2028.

Thousands of athletes and medical staff travel internationally for the Olympics.

Portable treatment systems that reduce travel weight and logistical complexity are highly valuable in that environment.

Sidekick Air has the potential to become a highly visible tool used in athlete recovery environments.

This kind of exposure would significantly accelerate adoption of the category.

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision for Sidekick Air and StitchCore is to establish a new family of inflatable structural support systems used across therapy, sports medicine, rescue operations, and consumer wellness.

Sidekick Air is the first step in that journey.
