# Sidekick Air Development Roadmap
Author: Drew Freedman
Date: March 2026

Purpose:
This document outlines the strategic execution roadmap for the Sidekick Air platform from prototype development to commercial launch and long-term expansion.

The roadmap is divided into phases:

1. Engineering Lock
2. Prototype Validation
3. Manufacturing Preparation
4. Market Launch
5. Platform Expansion

---

# Phase 1 — Engineering Lock
Timeline: Immediate – 90 Days

Goal:
Finalize the core engineering architecture so manufacturing and testing can proceed without ambiguity.

Key Tasks

Create the **Master Engineering Specification**

This document should define:

- exact material system
- valve specification
- seam welding method
- operating PSI range
- accessory interface standards
- stand geometry
- structural load targets

Resolve the **Stand Architecture**

The support stand must achieve:

- high stability
- lightweight construction
- rapid deployment
- compact folding

The stand decision is currently one of the most important engineering milestones.

Lock **Version 1 Product Scope**

Version 1 should include only essential components:

- inflatable tabletop
- collapsible stand
- face cradle attachment
- travel bag

Additional accessories should be introduced later.

Deliverables

- Master engineering specification
- Stand architecture decision
- Version 1 product definition

---

# Phase 2 — Prototype Validation
Timeline: 3–6 Months

Goal:
Demonstrate that the product performs reliably under real professional conditions.

Key Testing

Structural testing

- static load testing
- dynamic load testing
- edge load testing

Pressure testing

- pressure retention
- seam integrity
- inflation cycle durability

Field testing

- therapist usability
- setup time measurement
- stability evaluation
- outdoor use scenarios

Deliverables

Prototype Validation Report including:

- test data
- photos and videos
- therapist feedback
- performance benchmarks

This validation report becomes critical for:

- manufacturing partners
- investors
- licensing opportunities

---

# Phase 3 — Manufacturing Preparation
Timeline: 6–12 Months

Goal:
Establish a reliable manufacturing path for initial production.

Candidate Partners

- Wing Inflatables
- Third Dimension Textile Solutions
- Cooley Group

Key Activities

Material sourcing

Secure reliable supply for drop-stitch fabric.

Prototype manufacturing runs

Small batch production for refinement.

Cost analysis

Determine:

- bill of materials
- assembly cost
- packaging cost
- shipping cost

Quality control planning

Define:

- inspection standards
- pressure testing requirements
- durability standards

Deliverables

- manufacturing partner agreement
- production cost model
- pilot production plan

---

# Phase 4 — Market Launch
Timeline: 12–18 Months

Goal:
Introduce Sidekick Air into a focused professional market.

Primary Launch Market

Mobile massage therapists and sports recovery professionals.

This market has:

- strong portability needs
- high visibility environments
- rapid word-of-mouth potential

Launch Strategy

Demonstration marketing

Live demos at:

- athletic events
- race recovery tents
- therapy conferences

Influencer adoption

Target respected therapists and sports recovery practitioners.

Content marketing

Produce professional video demonstrations highlighting:

- portability
- setup speed
- structural stability

Direct sales channels

- online store
- professional distributor partnerships

Deliverables

- first production run
- professional demonstration content
- launch marketing campaign

# Key Success Factors

To achieve success, the project must focus on:

Engineering clarity

Avoid continuous design changes once the core product is locked.

Manufacturing reliability

Ensure consistent quality and scalability.

Market credibility

Demonstrate real professional use and performance.

Platform thinking

View Sidekick Air as the first product within a larger ecosystem.

---

# Summary

Sidekick Air has the potential to become the flagship product within a new category of portable rigid inflatable support platforms.

By focusing on:

clear engineering decisions  
validated performance  
strong manufacturing partners  
focused market entry

the platform can evolve from prototype concept into a scalable commercial product and long-term technology platform.
