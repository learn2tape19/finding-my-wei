# Founder's Address — Finding My Wei

**Author:** Drew Freedman, Steward  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Classification:** Constitutional (Foundational Philosophy)  
**Authority:** Founder

---

## To Those Who Steward This Work

If you are reading this, then Finding My Wei has outlived the moment in which it was created.

That alone is reason for gratitude.

This repository was never intended to become another collection of files, another knowledge base, or another digital archive. There are already countless places to store information. The world does not need one more.

Finding My Wei exists because information disappears, but wisdom often disappears faster.

Over the course of a lifetime, we accumulate experiences that quietly shape how we think, teach, invent, and serve others. Most of those lessons are never written down. They live in conversations, in failures, in moments of insight, and in decisions whose importance is only understood years later. When a career ends, those connections are often lost—not because they lacked value, but because no one preserved the reasoning behind them.

Finding My Wei was created to change that.

---

## Why This Institution Exists

The purpose of Finding My Wei is to preserve the evolution of understanding.

Its responsibility is not merely to remember what was created, but to preserve why it was created, what was learned, what changed, and how future generations might build upon those lessons.

This institution exists to transform experience into enduring intellectual assets.

Books.

Research.

Clinical insight.

Engineering.

Teaching.

Relationships.

Decisions.

Failures.

Successes.

Each becomes part of a larger conversation whose purpose is to help others think more clearly, care more deeply, and create more wisely.

---

## What We Believe

We believe that knowledge without context is fragile.

We believe that expertise should be shared rather than guarded.

We believe that thoughtful questions are often more valuable than immediate answers.

We believe that meaningful innovation comes from curiosity disciplined by evidence.

We believe that every profession advances when individuals freely contribute what they have learned.

Above all, we believe that wisdom grows through stewardship.

---

## What Must Never Change

This institution exists to serve people.

Architecture serves wisdom.

Wisdom never exists to serve architecture.

No system is more important than the work it exists to support.

No framework is more valuable than the people it ultimately helps.

No document is sacred simply because it was written.

The mission always comes before the mechanism.

---

## What Must Always Continue to Evolve

Science evolves.

Technology evolves.

Teaching evolves.

Clinical understanding evolves.

Businesses evolve.

Even deeply held beliefs should remain open to refinement when better evidence or better reasoning emerges.

Finding My Wei is intentionally designed to preserve principles while allowing understanding to mature.

Certainty is never the objective.

Learning is.

---

## The Responsibility of Stewardship

If you inherit this institution, remember that your responsibility is not to preserve everything.

It is to preserve what matters.

Do not mistake accumulation for wisdom.

Do not confuse complexity with depth.

Curate carefully.

Question respectfully.

Document thoughtfully.

Leave the institution clearer than you found it.

Every addition should strengthen the signal.

Every decision should reduce future uncertainty.

Every contribution should make it easier for those who follow.

---

## The Graduation Standard

Not every idea deserves permanence.

Many thoughts begin as possibilities.

Some become projects.

A few become contributions.

An intellectual asset earns its place within Finding My Wei only when it demonstrates enduring value through thoughtful review, meaningful application, and the possibility of helping others beyond its original context.

The purpose of this institution is not to remember everything.

It is to remember what should not be forgotten.

---

## Success

Success is not measured by the number of files preserved.

Nor by the number of projects completed.

Nor by the number of businesses created.

Success will be measured by whether someone, years from now, encounters an idea preserved here and is able to think more clearly, teach more effectively, solve a difficult problem, care for another person with greater wisdom, or continue a line of inquiry that otherwise would have been lost.

If that happens, then this institution has fulfilled its purpose.

---

## A Personal Reflection

My career has been shaped by remarkable teachers, generous colleagues, courageous patients, supportive family, trusted friends, and countless students who challenged me to continue learning.

Nothing preserved here was created in isolation.

Every page reflects conversations, mentorship, collaboration, and shared curiosity.

If my name appears throughout this repository, let it never imply ownership of ideas that were forged through the contributions of many others.

This institution is built in gratitude.

---

## To the Future

Do not protect this institution from change.

Protect it from forgetting why it exists.

Continue asking better questions.

Continue building bridges between disciplines.

Continue replacing certainty with understanding.

Continue serving people before systems.

And when the time comes, leave Finding My Wei stronger than you found it.

That is the responsibility—and the privilege—of stewardship.

---

**Drew Freedman**

**Founder, Steward**

**July 2026**
