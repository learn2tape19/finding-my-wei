## Purpose

Finding My Wei exists to create enduring intellectual work.

Producing work is not enough.

The institution must become more capable because of the work it produces.

Every project, publication, investigation, engineering effort, presentation, campaign, success, and failure should increase the institution's collective wisdom.

The institution learns.

The institution remembers.

The institution improves.

---

## Foundational Principle

**No institutional lesson should need to be learned twice.**

Every meaningful lesson shall become institutional knowledge.

Whenever practical, that knowledge should be documented, incorporated into operating procedures, and inherited by future work.

Experience should compound rather than repeat.

**Institutional memory shall always take precedence over individual memory.**

When an institutional standard exists, future work inherits it automatically without re-deliberation.

## Learning Cycle

Every meaningful project follows the same progression.

```
Observation
    ↓
Reflection
    ↓
Lesson
    ↓
Documentation
    ↓
Integration
    ↓
Institutional Standard
    ↓
Future Application
```

The goal is not simply to record what happened.

The goal is to permanently improve how the institution thinks.

## Operational Expectations

Whenever significant work concludes, ask:

- What did we learn?
- What assumptions changed?
- What surprised us?
- What should become permanent?
- What operating document should be updated?
- What future work should inherit this lesson?

If no permanent learning results, the review is incomplete.

## Department Responsibilities

### Editorial Board

Capture improvements in writing, communication, scientific framing, voice, and storytelling.

Update Editorial Standards when meaningful improvements are identified.

Maintain EDITORIAL_STANDARDS.md, VOICE_GUIDE.md, and related documents as living institutional records.

---

### Project Atlas

Capture research findings, methodological improvements, investigative techniques, and publication insights.

Update institutional research standards where appropriate.

Maintain decision logs for significant research directions.

---

### Engineering

Capture design decisions, prototype learnings, manufacturing insights, testing results, and validation procedures.

Maintain decision logs and engineering standards.

Document what works and why.

---

### Marketing

Capture campaign performance, messaging improvements, audience response, and communication strategies.

Improve future campaigns through documented learning.

Maintain campaign retrospectives and learnings.

---

### Founder

Evaluate whether important conversations should become lasting institutional knowledge.

Promote temporary insight into permanent understanding.

Authorize promotion of lessons into Constitutional or Operating Standards.

## Decision Logs

Each department should maintain an ongoing Decision Log.

Decision Logs preserve:

- The decision
- The reasoning
- The alternatives considered
- The expected outcome
- The lessons learned

Future contributors should understand not only what was decided, but why.

This prevents recapitulation of past deliberation and allows for principled evolution.

## Constitutional Standard

Whenever a recurring lesson is identified, it should be evaluated for promotion into one of the following:

- Constitutional Principles
- Operating Standards
- Departmental Procedures
- Editorial Standards
- Research Methodology
- Engineering Standards
- Publication Workflow
- Campaign Operations

This ensures valuable knowledge becomes part of the institution rather than remaining dependent upon memory.
