# Guiding Principle

## Institutional Evolution, NOT Institutional Replacement

Successful patterns are presumed valuable until proven otherwise.

**The burden of proof belongs to any recommendation that removes or replaces an existing organizational pattern.**

Decades of institutional practice represent wisdom.

That wisdom should be preserved and formalized, not discarded in favor of untested new architectures.

# Architectural Principles (Immutable)

**These principles may not be violated.**

### The Primacy of Execution

**Architecture is subordinate to execution.**

No future architectural changes may be proposed unless they solve a demonstrated operational problem.

**Rationale:** Architecture exists to serve work, not the reverse. Theoretical improvements without operational justification lead to churn, complexity, and institutional drift. Every architectural recommendation must begin with a concrete operational problem it solves.

This is the foundation of institutional discipline.

---

### GitHub Principle
GitHub preserves institutional memory.

It is the permanent record.

Every constitutional document, every strategic decision, every published work that must outlast the Founder belongs in GitHub.

### Obsidian Principle
Obsidian develops institutional understanding.

It is the Founder's thinking environment.

Connections, relationships, synthesis, and emerging understanding live here.

### Notion Principle
Notion executes institutional work.

It is the operational platform.

Projects, tasks, metrics, and workflows live here.

### Distinct Roles Principle
These roles must remain distinct.

No system should be asked to do all three.

Blurring these roles creates confusion and prevents each system from serving its purpose well.
