# Knowledge Graph Architecture

The conceptual design for how everything connects. Nothing exists in isolation.

This is not software. This is the **conceptual blueprint** for how ideas, projects, people, and principles relate to each other.

---

## The Seven Connection Points

Everything in Finding My Wei connects through seven node types:

### 1. Projects
Discrete bodies of work with clear boundaries.

**Examples**:
- The Tao of Clinical Touch
- Learn2Tape / K-Cuts
- Sidekick Air / StitchCore
- Therapeutic Alliance Research
- Finding My Wei itself

**Attributes**:
- Start date
- Owner(s)
- Status (active/completed/archived)
- Outcome/impact

**Connects to**: People (who contributed), Concepts (what it's based on), Principles (what it embodies), Research (what informed it), Products (what it created), Questions (what it answered)

---

### 2. People
Individuals who shaped thinking, collaborated, or influenced direction.

**Examples**:
- Whitney Lowe (Tao foreword)
- Collaborators and partners
- Customers and audience
- Mentors and teachers
- AI collaborators (Claude, ChatGPT)

**Attributes**:
- Name
- Relationship type
- Domain of expertise
- How they influenced
- Current status

**Connects to**: Projects (contributed to), Concepts (taught/influenced), Principles (embodied), Contact info and collaboration history

---

### 3. Concepts & Frameworks
Reusable ideas, frameworks, and intellectual property.

**Examples**:
- Permission-based approach
- Nervous system safety
- K-Cuts framework
- Therapeutic alliance
- Ethics as physiology
- UIP (Universal Insight Processor)

**Attributes**:
- Clear definition
- When it emerged
- Why it matters
- How it applies
- Evidence or backing

**Connects to**: Projects (applied in), People (originated by/taught by), Principles (related to), Research (studied by), Products (embodied in)

---

### 4. Principles
Timeless truths and operating rules.

**Examples**:
- Permanence > temporary
- Simplicity > complexity
- Reflection compounds
- Creation is emergence
- Ethics is physiology

**Attributes**:
- Core statement
- Why true
- Where it applies
- Exceptions/boundaries
- How to measure

**Connects to**: Concepts (builds on), Decisions (guides), Projects (governs), Research (validated by), Products (shapes)

---

### 5. Research
Investigations, findings, literature, and empirical work.

**Examples**:
- Therapeutic alliance research
- Nervous system safety literature
- Permission-based practice studies
- Ethics-physiology connection
- Clinical outcomes data

**Attributes**:
- Question being investigated
- Methodology
- Findings
- Sources and citations
- Applicability

**Connects to**: Concepts (explores), Projects (informs), Papers (supports), Questions (answers), Principles (validates/challenges)

---

### 6. Products & Creations
Tangible outputs and published work.

**Examples**:
- The Tao book (Amazon)
- Tao website (taoclinicaltouch.com)
- K-Cuts course (learn2tape.com)
- CE course (NCBTMB)
- Social media content
- Papers and articles
- Lectures and presentations
- Sidekick Air table

**Attributes**:
- Type (book, course, product, content)
- Platform/location
- Date created/published
- Audience
- Impact/reach

**Connects to**: Projects (created by), Concepts (embodies), Principles (demonstrates), People (contributed to), Products (built on)

---

### 7. Questions
Open investigations and strategic questions.

**Examples**:
- How does nervous system state affect outcomes?
- What research would strengthen Tao thesis?
- Where do Sidekick Air and Tao principles intersect?
- What's the teaching evolution path for CE course?

**Attributes**:
- The question
- Why it matters
- Current status (open/exploring/answered/archived)
- Related research
- Who's investigating

**Connects to**: Research (being investigated), Projects (raises the question), Concepts (explores), Principles (tests), Products (guides creation)

---

## Designing for Discovery

The Knowledge Graph is designed so you can **find** ideas, not just file them.

### Discovery Paths

**Start with a question**: "How is nervous system science relevant to my work?"
- вҶ’ Find Research on nervous system
- вҶ’ Find Concepts using that research
- вҶ’ Find Projects applying those concepts
- вҶ’ Find Products built from those projects
- вҶ’ Find People exploring this area
- вҶ’ Find related Questions

**Start with a person**: "What has Whitney Lowe influenced?"
- вҶ’ Find Projects Whitney contributed to
- вҶ’ Find Concepts Whitney teaches
- вҶ’ Find Research Whitney cites
- вҶ’ Find Products Whitney endorsed
- вҶ’ Find People in Whitney's network

**Start with a product**: "What ideas went into the K-Cuts course?"
- вҶ’ Find Project (Learn2Tape)
- вҶ’ Find Concepts (K-Cuts framework)
- вҶ’ Find Research (underpinning studies)
- вҶ’ Find Principles (ethical teaching)
- вҶ’ Find People (who built it)
- вҶ’ Find Questions (what students still ask)

## Connection Standards

### How to Make Connections

When creating a document, ask:

**What projects does this relate to?**
- Add links: `[[PROJECT_INDEX.md#project-name]]`

**What concepts does this build on?**
- Add links: `[[CONCEPT_INDEX.md#concept-name]]`

**What people contributed?**
- Add links: `[[PEOPLE_INDEX.md#person-name]]`

**What research supports this?**
- Add links: `[[07_Research/topic-name.md]]`

**What products exist around this?**
- Add links: `[[ASSET_INDEX.md#product-name]]`

**What questions does this explore?**
- Add links: `[[04_Decision_Framework/questions.md]]`

### Link Naming Convention

- Links use `[[descriptive-name]]` syntax
- The name matches the heading in the target file
- All links are searchable across the repository
- Broken links reveal gaps in the graph

---

## Maintaining the Graph

The graph grows as new work happens:

**When starting a project**: Document how it relates to existing concepts, people, principles

**When discovering a principle**: Link it to projects where it applies and people who embody it

**When publishing a product**: Connect it to the project, concepts, and research behind it

**When completing work**: Add to archive with full connection map showing what it touched

---

## Purpose

The Knowledge Graph ensures:

1. **Nothing is isolated** вҖ” Every insight connects to other work
2. **Discovery is possible** вҖ” You can find related ideas by following connections
3. **Learning compounds** вҖ” Seeing how ideas relate multiplies their value
4. **Future work is informed** вҖ” New projects see what came before
5. **Community possible** вҖ” Collaborators can understand the full context
